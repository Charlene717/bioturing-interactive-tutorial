STEP 2 · 品質管控

品質管控 (Quality Control)

在 BBrowserX 中，用小提琴圖與散佈圖「看見」資料品質，再以互動式滑桿設定過濾閾值——不寫任何程式碼。

In BBrowserX you "see" data quality with violin & scatter plots, then set filter thresholds interactively with sliders — no code required.

概覽

為什麼 QC 是第一件事？把它想成「細胞的健康檢查 + 夜店門禁」

想像你剛辦了一場超級盛大的派對（也就是你的定序實驗）。你的定序儀把幾萬顆細胞一顆一顆裝進微小的油滴 (droplet) 裡，理論上「一顆油滴 = 一顆細胞」。但現實很殘酷：有些油滴根本是空的、只裝了漂浮在液體裡的殘渣；有些細胞在被抓進去之前就已經半死不活；還有些油滴貪心地把兩顆細胞塞在一起。這些「混進來的雜客」就是我們必須在門口攔下的對象。

所以匯入資料後（見 Step 1），第一件永遠要做的事就是品質管控 (Quality Control)。你可以把 QC 想成兩件事的合體：一是幫每顆細胞做「健康檢查」（它還活著嗎？基因表現正常嗎？），二是當一名「夜店門禁」（活得好、來歷清楚的細胞放行；空袋子、快死的、擠兩個人進來的一律擋在門外）。若不先清理，這些「壞細胞」會在後續的降維、分群 (clustering) 與差異表達 (differential expression) 中製造出根本不存在的假群體，讓你把「垂死細胞」誤認為一種「新細胞型態」——這是初學者最常踩的坑。

BioTuring BBrowserX 把這整個流程視覺化、按鈕化：你不需要寫 subset() 或背任何 R 語法，而是在 GUI 中打開小提琴圖 (violin plot) 與散佈圖 (scatter plot)，親眼看見 QC 指標的分佈長什麼樣，再直接拖動閾值滑桿，即時看到有多少細胞被放行、多少被攔下。這正是 BBrowserX 的核心哲學：先互動地看見，再設閾值——絕不是背一組數字就套上去。

Picture the grandest party you've ever thrown (that's your sequencing experiment). The sequencer packs tens of thousands of cells, one at a time, into tiny oil droplets — in theory, "one droplet = one cell." But reality is messy: some droplets are empty, holding only debris floating in the buffer; some cells were already half-dead before they got captured; and some droplets greedily crammed two cells together. These "gate-crashers" are exactly who we need to stop at the door.

So after importing your data (see Step 1), the very first thing you always do is Quality Control (QC). Think of QC as two jobs fused together: a "health check-up" for every cell (is it alive? is its gene expression normal?), and a "nightclub door policy" (let in the healthy, well-behaved cells; turn away the empty bags, the dying, and the two-in-one crashers). If you don't clean up first, these "bad cells" will manufacture populations that don't actually exist in downstream dimensionality reduction, clustering, and differential expression — leading you to mistake a cluster of dying cells for a "new cell type." That's the single most common beginner trap.

BioTuring BBrowserX makes this whole flow visual and button-driven: instead of writing subset() or memorizing any R syntax, you open a violin plot and scatter plot in the GUI to actually see how your QC metrics are distributed, then drag threshold sliders to watch, in real time, how many cells get let in versus turned away. This is BBrowserX's core philosophy: visualize interactively first, then threshold — never just slap on a number you memorized.

💡
為什麼重要：QC 沒有放諸四海皆準的閾值。正確做法是先在 BBrowserX 打開小提琴圖 / 散佈圖，觀察你自己資料的分佈，再依物種、組織（例如心肌細胞粒線體天生偏高）與定序技術決定閾值。抄別人論文裡的「MT% < 5%」而不看圖，就像照別人的體檢報告吃自己的藥——很危險。想深入了解演算法細節，可對照我們的 scRNA-seq 互動教學 QC 章節。 Why it matters: there is no universal QC threshold. The right approach is to first open violin / scatter plots in BBrowserX and inspect your own data's distribution, then set thresholds according to species, tissue (e.g., cardiomyocytes are naturally high in mitochondria), and sequencing technology. Copying "MT% < 5%" from someone else's paper without looking at your plots is like taking medicine based on a stranger's health report — dangerous. 認識雜客

一、門口要攔下的三種「雜客」

在看指標之前，先認識我們到底在防誰。單細胞資料裡有三種典型的「壞客人」，每一種在後面的圖上都會留下獨特的「指紋」。認得它們，你之後看散佈圖就會像老鳥一樣一眼看穿。

Before we look at metrics, let's get to know who we're actually screening out. Single-cell data has three classic "bad guests," and each leaves a distinctive "fingerprint" on the plots you'll see later. Learn to recognize them, and you'll read a scatter plot like a seasoned pro.

🛍️

空液滴

油滴裡根本沒有完整細胞，只有漂在液體中的少量環境 RNA (ambient RNA)。就像有人買了門票、拎著購物袋進場，袋子裡卻空空如也。指紋：基因數極少、UMI 極少——擠在散佈圖左下角。

The droplet holds no intact cell at all — just a little ambient RNA drifting in the buffer. Like someone who bought a ticket and walked in carrying a shopping bag that turns out to be empty. Fingerprint: very few genes, very few UMIs — clustered in the lower-left of the scatter.

💀

垂死／死亡細胞

細胞膜破了，細胞質的 mRNA 漏光光，只剩下粒線體裡的 RNA 苟延殘喘。就像已經醉倒趴在沙發上、快被架出去的客人。指紋：高粒線體比例 (percent.mt)——像細胞發出的「求救信號」。

The membrane has broken, cytoplasmic mRNA has leaked out, and only mitochondrial RNA lingers on. Like the guest who's already passed out on the couch and is about to be carried out. Fingerprint: high mitochondrial fraction (percent.mt) — a cell's "distress signal."

👯

雙細胞 (doublet)

一顆油滴不小心抓進兩顆細胞，兩者的轉錄本被合併、貼上同一個條碼。就像兩個人硬擠著用一張門票混進場。指紋：基因數與 UMI 都異常偏高，可能同時表現本來不該共存的 marker（例如 T 細胞的 CD3D 又混著 B 細胞的 CD79A）。

One droplet accidentally captured two cells, whose transcripts got merged under a single barcode. Like two people squeezing through the door on one ticket. Fingerprint: abnormally high gene count AND UMI count, sometimes co-expressing markers that shouldn't coexist (e.g., T-cell CD3D mixed with B-cell CD79A).

🔎
實戰情境：假設你分析一份 10,000 顆細胞的 PBMC（周邊血單核細胞）資料。典型情況下，大約 5–10% 是空液滴、5–8% 是雙細胞（細胞裝載越多、雙細胞比例越高）、另有數個百分比是高 MT% 的垂死細胞。也就是說，一份看似「一萬顆細胞」的資料，真正乾淨可用的往往只有 8,000 顆上下——這就是 QC 存在的理由。 Real-world scenario: say you're analyzing a 10,000-cell PBMC (peripheral blood mononuclear cell) dataset. Typically ~5–10% are empty droplets, ~5–8% are doublets (the more cells you load, the higher the doublet rate), and a few more percent are high-MT% dying cells. So a dataset that looks like "10,000 cells" often yields only about 8,000 truly clean, usable cells — and that's exactly why QC exists.
核心指標

二、你要盯著的三個核心細胞指標

認識了雜客，接下來就是「怎麼抓到他們」。在 BBrowserX 匯入資料後，軟體會自動幫每顆細胞算好下面三個 QC 指標，並可一鍵畫成小提琴圖。整個門禁工作，九成都靠這三把尺：

Now that you know the crashers, the next question is "how do we catch them." After import, BBrowserX automatically computes the following three QC metrics for every cell and can plot them as violins in one click. Nine-tenths of the entire door policy runs on these three rulers:

🔢

nFeature_RNA

白話：這顆細胞裡「偵測到幾種不同的基因」（genes / cell）。像在檢查一個人的口袋裡有多少種類的東西。
• 過低（如 < 200）：口袋幾乎是空的 → 空液滴或破裂細胞，只捕到一點環境 RNA。
• 過高（如 > 5000，且 nCount 也高）：口袋塞爆了 → 很可能是雙細胞，兩顆細胞的基因合併撐大了數字。

Plain words: how many distinct genes were detected in this cell (genes / cell). Like counting how many kinds of things are in someone's pockets.
• Too low (e.g., < 200): nearly empty pockets → empty droplet or lysed cell, only a trace of ambient RNA.
• Too high (e.g., > 5000, with high nCount too): overstuffed pockets → likely a doublet, where two cells' genes merged and inflated the number.

📊

nCount_RNA

白話：這顆細胞抓到的「轉錄本分子總量」（總 UMI 數 / cell）。注意 UMI 數的是「幾個分子」，不是「幾種」——像數口袋裡總共有幾件東西。健康細胞的「種類數 (nFeature)」與「總量 (nCount)」會高度正相關，在散佈圖上沿對角線漂亮排開。偏離對角線、或異常偏高／偏低，通常就有品質問題。

Plain words: the total number of transcript molecules this cell captured (total UMIs / cell). Note that UMI counts how many molecules, not how many kinds — like counting the total number of items in the pockets. In healthy cells, "kinds (nFeature)" and "total (nCount)" are strongly correlated, lining up neatly along the diagonal of a scatter. Cells off the diagonal, or abnormally high/low, usually have a quality problem.

🔋

percent.mt

白話：粒線體基因佔比 (mitochondrial %)——判斷細胞死活最靈敏的一把尺，也是那個「求救信號」。細胞破裂時細胞質 mRNA 流失，但粒線體 RNA 被雙層膜牢牢護住而殘留，於是分母縮小、MT% 飆高。所以 高 MT% ≈ 垂死／死亡細胞。BBrowserX 依 MT-（人）/mt-（鼠）基因前綴自動計算。

Plain words: the mitochondrial gene percentage (mitochondrial %) — the most sensitive ruler for viability, and that "distress signal." When a cell ruptures, cytoplasmic mRNA leaks away, but mitochondrial RNA is shielded by a double membrane and stays behind, so the denominator shrinks and MT% spikes. Hence high MT% ≈ dying/dead cells. BBrowserX computes it automatically from the MT- (human) / mt- (mouse) gene prefix.

🧠
專家小撇步：搞懂「為什麼」高 MT% 就代表死亡，你才不會誤殺好細胞。關鍵在那層雙層膜。想像細胞是一顆裝滿水的氣球（外膜），裡面還有幾顆小水球（粒線體，各自有雙層膜）。氣球一破，大部分的水（細胞質 mRNA）嘩啦流光；但裡面那幾顆小水球比較耐操，還撐著沒破。結果被測到的 RNA 裡，粒線體的占比就變得異常高。但要小心：心肌細胞、肝細胞、棕色脂肪這類「能量工廠」型組織，本來活著的時候粒線體就特別多，MT% 天生就偏高——這時候硬套 5% 會把一堆健康細胞冤枉成死細胞。 Pro tip: understand *why* high MT% signals death, so you don't kill good cells by mistake. The key is that double membrane. Picture a cell as a water balloon (outer membrane) with a few tiny water balloons inside (mitochondria, each with its own double membrane). Pop the big balloon and most of the water (cytoplasmic mRNA) gushes out — but those little inner balloons are tougher and hold on. So among the RNA you still detect, the mitochondrial share becomes abnormally high. But beware: "energy-factory" tissues like cardiomyocytes, hepatocytes, and brown fat carry loads of mitochondria even when perfectly alive, so their MT% is naturally high — force a 5% cutoff there and you'll wrongly condemn scores of healthy cells as dead.

🚫
常見誤區：只看單一指標。光看 nFeature 或光看 MT% 都會漏抓或誤殺。例如：一顆基因數中等、UMI 也中等，看起來很正常的細胞，若 MT% 高達 20%，它其實是垂死細胞；反過來，一顆 MT% 正常但 nFeature/nCount 雙雙爆高的細胞，很可能是雙細胞。三把尺要一起看，這正是散佈圖比小提琴圖更強大的原因——下一節見真章。 Common mistake: judging by one metric alone. Looking only at nFeature, or only at MT%, will make you miss bad cells or kill good ones. Example: a cell with moderate genes and moderate UMIs looks perfectly normal — but if its MT% hits 20%, it's actually dying. Conversely, a cell with normal MT% but both nFeature and nCount sky-high is very likely a doublet. Read all three rulers together — which is exactly why the scatter plot beats the violin plot. 聯合分佈的威力

三、為什麼「散佈圖對角線」比單一指標更會抓人

小提琴圖一次只看一把尺，就像門口只檢查「身高」——你會放行一個很高但明顯醉倒的人。散佈圖則同時看兩把尺（nCount × nFeature），一下子把每顆細胞的「個性」攤開。健康細胞有一個很美的性質：基因種類越多，分子總量也越多，兩者成比例，於是它們會乖乖沿著一條對角線 (diagonal) 排成一片雲。這條對角線就是「正常人的隊伍」。

真正的壞細胞往往就是那些脫離對角線的離群點：

  • 對角線左下角那一坨：低 nFeature + 低 nCount → 空液滴 / 破裂細胞。
  • 對角線右上角飄出去的：超高 nFeature + 超高 nCount → 疑似雙細胞。
  • 對角線下方偏離的點：nCount 不低、nFeature 卻偏少（分子很多但種類很少）→ 可能是特殊細胞（如紅血球被血紅素基因灌爆），或技術假象。

這種「靠關係、不靠單一數字」的思維，就是專家和新手最大的差別。BBrowserX 讓你直接在散佈圖上框選 (gating) 出對角線那片健康雲，門禁工作瞬間變得直覺又精準。

A violin plot looks at one ruler at a time — like a bouncer checking only "height," who'd wave through a very tall man who's obviously passed out. A scatter plot uses two rulers at once (nCount × nFeature), instantly revealing each cell's "personality." Healthy cells have a lovely property: the more gene kinds they have, the more total molecules too — the two scale together, so they line up along a tidy diagonal as a dense cloud. That diagonal is "the line of normal folks."

The truly bad cells are usually the outliers that fall off the diagonal:

  • The blob at the lower-left: low nFeature + low nCount → empty droplets / lysed cells.
  • The stragglers drifting off the upper-right: very high nFeature + very high nCount → suspected doublets.
  • Points sagging below the diagonal: nCount not low but nFeature unusually small (lots of molecules, few kinds) → possibly a special cell type (e.g., red blood cells flooded with hemoglobin genes) or a technical artifact.

This "reason from relationships, not from a single number" mindset is the biggest difference between an expert and a novice. BBrowserX lets you gate the healthy diagonal cloud directly on the scatter, making the whole door policy instantly intuitive and precise.

📐
專家小撇步：很多人喜歡在散佈圖上多加一條輔助資訊——把每個點的顏色或大小對應到 MT%。這樣一來，那些「明明在對角線上、看似健康、卻紅通通（高 MT%）」的細胞就會現形。下一節的互動模擬器就是這樣設計的：點越大 = MT% 越高，你會親眼看到潛伏在健康雲裡的死細胞。 Pro tip: many people layer one more signal onto the scatter — mapping each dot's color or size to MT%. That way, cells that "sit right on the diagonal, look healthy, yet glow red (high MT%)" reveal themselves. The interactive simulator in the next section is built exactly this way: bigger dot = higher MT%, so you'll literally see dead cells lurking inside the healthy cloud.
互動模擬

四、互動：親手當一次「細胞門禁」

紙上談兵到此為止——現在換你上工。這個模擬器重現 BBrowserX 的操作感受：拖動滑桿設定 Min nFeature（門檻太低的空液滴擋掉）、Max nFeature（門檻太高的雙細胞擋掉）與 Max MT%（求救信號太強的死細胞擋掉），右方即時更新保留 / 過濾細胞數與保留率。散佈圖呈現 nCount × nFeature 聯合分佈（綠 = 放行、紅 = 攔下），點越大代表 MT% 越高；下方三張分佈圖對應各指標。試著把 Max MT% 從 25% 一路拉到 5%，看那些藏在對角線上的大紅點如何被揪出來。切記：「過濾」只代表「在當前閾值下被移除」，不等於該細胞一定是壞的——請配合生物學情境判斷。

🎯
實戰情境：找出你的「甜蜜點」。試試看：把 Min nFeature 拉到 500、Max MT% 壓到 3%，你會發現保留率驟降——過濾太狠，連健康的靜止期小細胞都被冤殺了。反過來全部放到最寬，紅點幾乎消失，但你也把死細胞和雙細胞放進場了。好的門禁是剛好攔住三坨離群點、又不動到對角線主雲的那個平衡。多數 PBMC 資料的常見起點大約落在 nFeature 200–5000、MT% < 10%，但請務必以你自己的圖為準。 Real-world scenario: find your "sweet spot." Try this: push Min nFeature to 500 and squeeze Max MT% to 3%, and watch retention crater — filter too hard and you kill even healthy, quiescent small cells. Now open everything wide and the red almost vanishes — but you've just let dead cells and doublets into the party. Good bouncing is the balance that catches exactly the three outlier blobs while leaving the main diagonal cloud untouched. A common starting point for PBMC data is roughly nFeature 200–5000 and MT% < 10% — but always defer to your own plots.
基因層級與假象

五、基因層級過濾與技術性假象

到目前為止我們都在「篩細胞」（篩客人）。但別忘了還有另一個方向的清理：「篩基因」。想像資料是一張超大的 Excel 表，橫列是細胞、直行是基因。我們不只刪掉爛的橫列（壞細胞），也要刪掉幾乎全是 0 的直行（沒用的基因）。匯入時 BBrowserX 通常提供「最少細胞數 (min cells)」門檻——只在極少數細胞出現的基因多半是雜訊或定序假象，剔除它們可降低運算負擔、減少統計雜訊，也讓後續分群更乾淨。

So far we've been "filtering cells" (screening guests). But don't forget the other cleanup direction: "filtering genes." Picture the data as a giant Excel sheet — rows are cells, columns are genes. We remove not only the bad rows (bad cells) but also the columns that are almost entirely zeros (useless genes). At import BBrowserX typically offers a "min cells" threshold — genes seen in only a handful of cells are usually noise or sequencing artifacts, and removing them reduces compute burden, cuts statistical noise, and makes downstream clustering cleaner.

🧬 基因層級：最少細胞數

常見做法是剔除「在少於 3 個細胞中表現」的基因。想想看：一個基因如果在一萬顆細胞裡只有 2 顆偵測到，它幾乎不可能在下游提供任何統計顯著性，反而拖慢運算、放大雜訊。BBrowserX 於匯入 / 過濾步驟提供這個門檻選項。但別把門檻設太高——某些真正稀有的細胞型態 marker 本來就只在少數細胞出現，設得太嚴會連它們一起清掉。

A common rule removes genes detected in fewer than ~3 cells. Think about it: a gene seen in only 2 of 10,000 cells can almost never yield statistical significance downstream — it just slows compute and amplifies noise. BBrowserX exposes this threshold at the import / filtering step. But don't set it too high — some genuinely rare cell-type markers appear in only a few cells by nature, and an overly strict cutoff wipes them out too.

👯 雙細胞與環境 RNA（那鍋「湯」）

殘酷的真相：單靠 nFeature／nCount 上限抓不乾淨兩顆小細胞組成的雙細胞——它們的合併數字可能剛好落在正常範圍裡。另外，整鍋反應液裡漂著破裂細胞漏出的游離 mRNA，這鍋環境 RNA（「soup」）會被隨機分裝進每顆油滴，讓細胞「莫名其妙」表現一些根本不屬於它的基因（例如非免疫細胞卻測到一點血紅素）。程式碼世界用 Scrublet / DoubletFinder（雙細胞）與 SoupX / CellBender（環境 RNA）專門對付——就算在無程式碼的 BBrowserX 裡，也要對這些假象保持警覺。

The hard truth: upper limits on nFeature/nCount can't fully catch doublets made of two small cells — their merged numbers may land right inside the normal range. On top of that, free-floating mRNA leaked from ruptured cells drifts through the whole reaction mix; this ambient RNA ("soup") gets randomly parceled into every droplet, making cells "inexplicably" express genes that aren't theirs (e.g., a non-immune cell showing a whiff of hemoglobin). In code these are handled by dedicated tools — Scrublet / DoubletFinder (doublets) and SoupX / CellBender (ambient RNA) — so stay aware of these artifacts even in a no-code GUI.

👻
常見誤區：以為「篩過細胞就沒事了」。環境 RNA 這鍋湯不會被 nFeature/MT% 過濾掉，它是「均勻污染」——每顆細胞都沾一點。它最陰險的後果是讓兩群其實不同的細胞在分群時「假裝相似」，或讓 marker 基因看起來到處都有微弱表現。若你發現「幾乎每一群都表現某個高丰度基因（如血紅素 HBB）」，先別急著下生物學結論——很可能是 soup 在搗亂。 Common mistake: assuming "filtering cells is enough." The ambient RNA soup won't be removed by nFeature/MT% filters — it's "uniform contamination," a little smeared onto every cell. Its most insidious effect is making two genuinely different populations "pretend to be similar" during clustering, or making marker genes look faintly expressed everywhere. If you notice "nearly every cluster expresses some high-abundance gene (e.g., hemoglobin HBB)," don't rush to a biological conclusion — it's very likely the soup causing mischief.

閾值策略

六、閾值怎麼設？固定 vs 自適應

門禁的鬆緊沒有標準答案，但有一套可靠的「決策心法」。與其死背數字，不如照著下面這棵決策樹一步步問自己。

There's no single right setting for how tight to run the door — but there is a reliable "decision playbook." Rather than memorizing numbers, walk yourself through the tree below step by step.

🌳 閾值決策流程

Q1:
先畫小提琴圖 / 散佈圖了嗎？ → 否 → 先畫再說。絕不要在沒看過分佈前套用別人的數字。
Q2:

🧮
白話解釋 MAD：MAD（中位數絕對偏差, Median Absolute Deviation）就是「大家離中位數平均差多遠」的一把穩健尺——比標準差更不怕極端值搗亂。「中位數 ± 3×MAD」的意思是：離群超過 3 個 MAD 就當作異常。它的美妙之處在於讓資料自己決定門檻：品質好的樣本尺會收緊，品質差的樣本尺會放寬，你不必為每個樣本手動猜數字。這也是為什麼多樣本整合分析特別推薦它。 MAD in plain words: MAD (Median Absolute Deviation) is a robust ruler for "how far, on average, everyone sits from the median" — far less rattled by extreme values than standard deviation. "Median ± 3×MAD" means: anything more than 3 MADs out counts as abnormal. Its beauty is that it lets the data set its own cutoff: the ruler tightens for a clean sample and loosens for a messy one, so you don't hand-guess a number per sample. That's why it's especially recommended for multi-sample integration.
⚠️
專家五原則（貼在牆上）：
① 沒有放諸四海皆準的閾值。
② 聯合分佈（散佈圖）比單一指標更有用——好細胞沿對角線。
③ 寧可稍寬鬆也別過度過濾——過嚴可能刪掉稀有亞群。
④ 別忘了 soup 與雙細胞——它們躲得過簡單閾值。
⑤ 把 QC 當迭代過程——先寬鬆分群，若某 cluster 全是高 MT% 且無特異 marker，再回頭收緊。
Five expert rules (pin to the wall):
① No universal thresholds exist.
② Joint distributions (scatter) beat single metrics — good cells follow the diagonal.
③ Better slightly lenient than over-filtered — strict QC may delete rare subpopulations.
④ Don't forget soup & doublets — they slip past simple thresholds.
⑤ Treat QC as iterative — cluster leniently first; if a cluster is all high-MT% with no specific markers, go back and tighten.
