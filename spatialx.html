SPATIAL · 空間生物學

SpatialX：在組織脈絡中看細胞

當「細胞在哪裡」和「細胞是什麼」同樣重要——認識 BioTuring 的空間分析平台（前身 Lens）。

When "where a cell is" matters as much as "what a cell is" — meet BioTuring's spatial platform (formerly Lens).

核心觀念

一、空間分析多帶來了什麼？

先講個生活比喻。做傳統 scRNA-seq，就像你想調查一座城市的居民組成，於是把整座城市的人打散、全部抓到同一個大廳裡填問卷——你能問清楚每個人的職業、年齡、興趣（也就是每顆細胞的完整轉錄組），統計出「這座城有 30% 上班族、20% 學生、15% 醫護……」。問卷做得很細，但你付出了一個代價：大家的住址全被打亂了。你再也不知道誰住在河邊、誰住市中心、哪兩個人本來是隔壁鄰居。

這正是 scRNA-seq 的解離 (dissociate) 步驟——把組織用酵素消化成單細胞懸浮液。這一步讓我們測到每顆細胞的完整轉錄組，卻也永久丟失了每顆細胞原本在組織裡的位置與鄰里關係。你知道「有哪些細胞、各佔多少」，卻答不出「它們排在哪、和誰做鄰居」。

空間轉錄組 (spatial transcriptomics) 換了一種問法：它不打散城市，而是改看一張標了每個人住址的衛星地圖。每顆細胞（或每個測量點）在切片上的座標都被完整保留，於是「WHERE（在哪裡）」這個維度回來了。你可以把細胞放回組織結構 (tissue architecture) 的脈絡中重新詮釋：這群 T 細胞是散落各處，還是沿著腫瘤邊界排成一道防線？某個分泌訊號分子的細胞，是否正好緊貼著帶有對應受體的細胞？這些「誰在哪、誰貼著誰」的問題，解離式資料永遠答不了。

SpatialX 就是把「細胞身份 (what)」與「空間位置 (where)」重新縫合起來的無程式碼 (no-code) 平台——你在同一個介面裡，既做分群與細胞註釋，也做鄰近細胞、空間區域與原位細胞互動的分析。它是 BioTuring 空間生物學工具的最新一代，前身叫做 Lens。

Start with an everyday picture. Classic scRNA-seq is like surveying a city's residents by rounding every person up, scattering them, and herding them into one big hall to fill out a questionnaire. You can ask each person about their job, age, and hobbies in exquisite detail (each cell's full transcriptome) and tally that "this city is 30% office workers, 20% students, 15% healthcare…". The survey is thorough — but at a price: everyone's home address has been scrambled. You can no longer tell who lived by the river, who lived downtown, or which two people used to be next-door neighbors.

That is exactly the dissociation step in scRNA-seq — enzymatically digesting tissue into a single-cell suspension. It lets us profile each cell's full transcriptome, but it permanently discards where each cell sat and who its neighbors were. You learn "which cell types are present and in what proportion," but you cannot answer "where they sit and who they neighbor."

Spatial transcriptomics asks the question differently: instead of scattering the city, it reads a satellite map with every resident's address marked on it. The coordinates of every cell (or measurement spot) on the section are preserved, so the "WHERE" dimension comes back. You can reinterpret cells in the context of tissue architecture: are these T cells scattered, or lined up like a wall along the tumor boundary? Is the cell secreting a signal actually pressed against the cell bearing its receptor? These "who's where, who touches whom" questions are ones dissociated data can never answer.

SpatialX is the no-code platform that stitches cell identity (what) back together with spatial position (where) — in one interface you cluster and annotate, and also analyze neighborhoods, spatial domains, and in-situ cell interactions. It is the latest generation of BioTuring's spatial tooling; its predecessor was called Lens.

💡
一句話記法：scRNA-seq 告訴你「有什麼細胞」；空間分析再加上「它們排在哪、和誰做鄰居」。SpatialX 的所有進階功能，都是圍繞這個「鄰居」概念展開的——記住「鄰居」二字，後面就都通了。 One-liner: scRNA-seq tells you which cells exist; spatial adds where they sit and who their neighbors are. Every advanced SpatialX feature builds on that idea of neighbors — hold onto that word and the rest falls into place.
🎯

為什麼重要：腫瘤邊界

腫瘤是否已經「突破邊界、往外侵犯」，是判斷惡性程度的關鍵。只有保留位置，你才看得到癌細胞是否越過基底膜、和正常組織如何交界。

Whether a tumor has "broken through its margin and invaded outward" is central to grading malignancy. Only with position preserved can you see whether cancer cells cross the basement membrane and how they interface with normal tissue.

🛡️

為什麼重要：免疫浸潤

同樣「有很多 T 細胞」，一種是 T 細胞真的鑽進腫瘤核心（熱腫瘤，免疫療法多半有效），一種是被擋在腫瘤外圍（冷腫瘤／免疫排除）。位置直接關係到能否用免疫療法。

Two tumors can both have "lots of T cells," yet one has T cells that truly penetrate the core (a hot tumor, often immunotherapy-responsive) while the other keeps them parked at the rim (a cold / immune-excluded tumor). Position directly informs whether immunotherapy can work.

🧱

為什麼重要：組織分層

大腦皮質有六層、腸壁有黏膜到肌層、腎臟有皮質與髓質。這些「分層 (layering)」結構決定功能——沒有座標，你根本畫不出這些層。

The cortex has six layers, the gut wall runs mucosa to muscularis, the kidney has cortex and medulla. This layering defines function — and without coordinates you cannot draw these layers at all.

支援平台

二、SpatialX 支援哪些空間技術？

空間技術聽起來品牌繁多，但只要抓住一個核心取捨就不會迷路：「空間看得多細」和「基因看得多全」，這兩件事永遠在拔河。因此所有技術大致落在兩大家族裡，SpatialX 兩者都支援：

The brand names look overwhelming, but one core trade-off keeps you oriented: "how finely you see space" and "how broadly you see genes" are always in a tug-of-war. So every technology falls into one of two families — and SpatialX supports both:

🟦

(a) 定點／區域為主

10x Visium（組織級、定點）、NanoString GeoMx DSP（使用者親手圈選 ROI 區域）。可以想成把切片鋪上一層棋盤格，每一格量一次。問題是一格常常住了好幾個人——一個「點 (spot)」或「區域」內通常含多顆細胞，並非單細胞解析度。所以想知道「這格裡各有多少種細胞」，需要 deconvolution（去卷積） 這個「拆帳」步驟去推估各細胞類型的比例。它最大的優點是全轉錄組覆蓋 (whole-transcriptome)——不預先挑基因，兩萬多個基因通通量。

10x Visium (tissue-level, spot-based) and NanoString GeoMx DSP (regions the user draws by hand as ROIs). Picture laying a chessboard grid over the section and measuring each square once. The catch: each square usually houses several people — a "spot" or region typically holds multiple cells, not single-cell resolution. To learn "how many of each cell type live in this square," you need deconvolution, a "splitting-the-bill" step that estimates the cell-type proportions. Its biggest strength: whole-transcriptome coverage — no pre-selection; all ~20,000+ genes are measured.

🔬

(b) 單細胞／次細胞成像

10x Xenium、NanoString CosMx、Vizgen MERFISH。這一家用顯微成像直接「看」每一個 RNA 分子亮起來，達到真正的單細胞、甚至次細胞 (subcellular) 解析度——你能看到單一 transcript 落在哪顆細胞的哪個位置。代價是它只問「名單上的人」：使用靶向基因套組 (targeted panel)，只測預先選定的數百到數千個基因，沒被選進名單的基因，這台機器根本不會去看。

10x Xenium, NanoString CosMx, Vizgen MERFISH. This family uses microscopy to literally "watch" each RNA molecule light up, reaching true single-cell, even subcellular resolution — you can see a single transcript land inside a given cell. The cost: it only asks about "people on the list" — a targeted gene panel of hundreds to a few thousand pre-selected genes. Anything off the list is simply never looked at.

此外還有第三種「折衷派」：Slide-seq（珠子為基礎的高解析度定點技術），解析度比 Visium 更細、更接近單細胞，但骨子裡仍屬定點式、走全轉錄組路線——它也在 SpatialX 支援之列。

There is also a third "compromise" option: Slide-seq (a bead-based, high-resolution spot technology). Its resolution is finer than Visium and closer to single-cell, but at heart it is still spot-based and whole-transcriptome — and it too is supported by SpatialX.

📝
記憶取捨（一句就懂）：定點技術＝基因看得全，但空間看不細；成像技術＝空間看得細（單細胞），但基因看得少（靶向）。沒有一種技術能兩者兼得，選哪一家永遠取決於你的科學問題——想「大海撈針找未知」選定點，想「精準定位已知」選成像。 Remember the trade-off (in one line): spot methods = sees genes broadly, space coarsely; imaging methods = sees space finely (single-cell), genes narrowly (targeted). No technology has both — the choice always follows your question: "cast a wide net for the unknown" → spot; "pinpoint the known" → imaging.
🧑‍🏫
專家小撇步：一個常見的實戰組合是「先廣後窄」——先用 Visium 全轉錄組掃過整片組織、找出哪些基因與區域值得關注，再設計一組靶向套組上 Xenium／CosMx，在單細胞解析度上把重點區域看仔細。兩種資料都能匯進 SpatialX 對照著看。 Pro tip: a common real-world combo is "wide first, then narrow" — sweep the whole section with Visium's whole-transcriptome view to find which genes and regions matter, then design a targeted panel for Xenium/CosMx to examine the key regions at single-cell resolution. Both datasets can be brought into SpatialX and compared side by side.
互動：平台選擇器

三、點一個技術，看它的特性

光看文字容易糊成一團，動手點點看最快。選一個空間技術，SpatialX 會告訴你它的解析度（定點 vs 單細胞）、基因套組（全轉錄組 vs 靶向）、家族與典型用途。一邊點一邊在心裡問自己：這台適合「找未知」還是「定位已知」？

Words blur together; clicking is faster. Pick a spatial technology and SpatialX shows its resolution (spot vs single-cell), panel type (whole-tx vs targeted), family, and typical use. As you click, ask yourself: is this one built for "finding the unknown" or "pinpointing the known"?

Below is a synthetic tissue section: each dot is a cell, positioned by its coordinates on the slide. This is what makes spatial data so compelling — the same coordinates let you ask two completely different questions. Toggle the buttons to feel it: first view the cell-type distribution (which people), then the spatial niche (which region forms one community).

合成示意資料，非真實切片。「細胞類型」模式回答『有哪些細胞』——三種細胞散佈全圖；「鄰域」模式回答『哪些區塊由相似鄰居構成一個社區』——同一份座標，兩種問法。試著切換，注意腫瘤細胞（紅）與免疫細胞（藍）在鄰域模式下如何被劃進不同的『社區』。

💡
怎麼看：在「細胞類型」模式下你看到三種細胞散佈全圖，這是 scRNA-seq 也做得到的層次（只是它畫的是 UMAP，不是真實座標）。切到「鄰域」模式，SpatialX 會依每顆細胞周圍的鄰居組成把切片分成幾個空間區域 (niche/domain)——腫瘤核心、免疫浸潤帶、基質區。這種「組織區塊」正是空間分析獨有、解離式資料絕對生不出來的產出。 How to read it: in "cell type" mode you see three cell types spread across the image — a level scRNA-seq can also reach (except it plots a UMAP, not real coordinates). Switch to "niche" mode and SpatialX partitions the section into spatial domains based on the neighborhood composition around each cell: tumor core, immune-infiltrated rim, stromal zone. These "tissue blocks" are output unique to spatial analysis and something dissociated data can never produce.
🔎
實戰情境：假設紅色是癌細胞、藍色是 T 細胞。在鄰域模式下，如果 T 細胞的藍點大量落在腫瘤核心 niche 裡，代表免疫細胞成功浸潤（熱腫瘤）；如果藍點只圍在免疫浸潤帶而進不了核心，就是被排除在外的冷腫瘤。這個「一眼看出浸潤與否」的判讀，就是空間分析在腫瘤免疫學裡最實用的價值。 Real-world scenario: say red is cancer cells and blue is T cells. In niche mode, if the blue T-cell dots land heavily inside the tumor-core niche, immune cells have successfully infiltrated (a hot tumor); if the blue dots only ring the immune-rim and can't reach the core, it's an excluded, cold tumor. This at-a-glance "infiltrated or not" read-out is spatial analysis's most practical payoff in tumor immunology.
工作流程

五、SpatialX 的典型流程

SpatialX 為無程式碼、Windows 版的桌面工具。好消息是：流程的前半段和你已經學過的 scRNA-seq 幾乎一模一樣，你不必從零學起；真正的新東西集中在後半段的「空間專屬」分析。把它想成「熟悉的前菜 + 全新的主餐」：

SpatialX is a no-code, Windows-based desktop tool. The good news: the first half of the workflow is almost identical to the scRNA-seq you already learned — no need to start over; the genuinely new material lives in the "spatial-specific" second half. Think of it as "a familiar appetizer plus a brand-new main course":

從載入到原位互動

1. 載入資料 + 影像：匯入表達矩陣以及對應的組織影像／座標。這是空間流程最關鍵、也最容易被忽略的一步——切片影像不是漂亮的裝飾，它是「地圖底圖」，之後每一個空間分析都要靠它定位。

2. 品質管制 (QC)：過濾低品質的定點／細胞。除了 scRNA 常見的「計數過低」外，成像資料還要特別留意細胞分割 (segmentation) 失敗的細胞（邊界切壞、面積異常）。

3. 分群與註釋：做分群並指定細胞類型。這裡有個省時神招——可從已註釋好的 scRNA 參考資料轉移標籤 (label transfer)，讓只測了幾百個基因的靶向空間資料，直接「借用」完整 scRNA 圖譜的細胞類型定義，不必從頭手動命名。

4. 空間專屬分析（主餐上桌）： ① 空間區域／niche（社區）——依每顆細胞的鄰居組成，把切片分成有意義的組織區塊； ② 鄰近分析／細胞類型共定位 (co-localization)——回答「誰跟誰住一起」，哪些細胞類型愛聚在同一區； ③ 原位細胞互動 (in-situ cell–cell interaction)——在真實空間中檢查 ligand–receptor 訊號：分泌訊號的細胞，是否真的緊鄰帶著受體的細胞？

1. Load data + image: import the expression matrix and its matching tissue image/coordinates. This is the most crucial and most overlooked step of a spatial workflow — the section image isn't pretty decoration, it's the "base map," and every spatial analysis afterward relies on it to place things.

2. QC: filter low-quality spots/cells. Beyond the usual scRNA "too-few counts," imaging data needs special attention to cells with failed segmentation (broken boundaries, abnormal areas).

3. Cluster & annotate: cluster and assign cell types. Here's a time-saver — you can transfer labels from an already-annotated scRNA reference (label transfer), letting targeted spatial data measuring only a few hundred genes directly "borrow" the cell-type definitions from a full scRNA atlas instead of naming everything by hand.

4. Spatial-specific analyses (the main course): ① spatial domains / niches (communities) — partition the section into meaningful tissue blocks by each cell's neighborhood composition; ② neighborhood / cell-type co-localization — answers "who lives with whom," which cell types cluster in the same region; ③ in-situ cell–cell interaction — check ligand–receptor signaling in real space: is the signal-secreting cell truly adjacent to the receptor-bearing one?

三大空間分析

六、「主餐」三道菜：把空間問題拆開講

流程第 4 步那三種「空間專屬」分析，是初學者最容易混淆的地方。其實它們回答的是三個層層遞進的問題，用一句社區生活的話就講清楚了：

The three "spatial-specific" analyses in step 4 are where beginners most often get muddled. In truth they answer three questions that build on each other — and one line of neighborhood-life language makes each clear:

🏘️

① Niche／空間區域

「這一區是哪種社區？」把地圖依鄰里組成分成幾個街區——住宅區、商業區、工業區。在組織裡就是腫瘤核心、免疫帶、基質區。它切的是區域，不是單一細胞。

"What kind of neighborhood is this area?" Carve the map into districts by their mix of residents — residential, commercial, industrial. In tissue: tumor core, immune band, stromal zone. It slices regions, not single cells.

🤝

② 共定位／鄰近分析

「誰跟誰住一起？」統計哪兩種細胞類型出現在彼此身邊的頻率，是否高於隨機。例如「巨噬細胞總是黏在腫瘤細胞旁」就是一個共定位訊號。

"Who lives next to whom?" Quantify how often two cell types appear beside each other, above chance. "Macrophages always cling to tumor cells" is exactly such a co-localization signal.

📡

③ 原位細胞互動

「鄰居之間真的在對話嗎？」不只看住在一起，還檢查一方是否分泌訊號 (ligand)、另一方是否帶著接收器 (receptor)。空間版比解離版更可信——因為訊號真的要「碰得到」才算數。

"Are the neighbors actually talking?" Beyond living together, check whether one side secretes a signal (ligand) and the other bears the receiver (receptor). The spatial version is more trustworthy than the dissociated one — because a signal only counts if it can physically "reach."

🧑‍🏫
專家小撇步：細胞通訊分析（如 CellChat）在解離式 scRNA-seq 裡是「憑基因表達推測誰可能跟誰講話」，但它不知道兩種細胞是不是真的挨在一起。空間資料把這個弱點補起來了——原位互動＝「有訊號＋距離夠近」兩個條件同時成立，可信度高得多。這是空間分析最能加值細胞通訊研究的地方。 Pro tip: in dissociated scRNA-seq, cell-communication analysis (e.g., CellChat) infers "who might talk to whom" from gene expression alone — but it has no idea whether the two cell types are actually next to each other. Spatial data fixes that weakness: in-situ interaction requires both "a signal" and "close enough distance" at once, which is far more credible. This is where spatial analysis adds the most value to cell-communication studies.

三個常見誤區（新手必踩，先看先避）： ① 把 Visium spot 當單細胞——這是最常見的錯。一個 spot 裡住了好幾顆、甚至十幾顆細胞，它的表達譜是混合訊號。把一個 spot 直接當成「一顆細胞」去做單細胞分析，結論會被稀釋、被汙染。想要細胞比例，得靠 deconvolution 去卷積推估，而且那是估計值，別在邊界上過度解讀。 ② targeted panel 沒有的基因，誤當成「不表現」——成像技術只測套組內的幾百個基因。你「沒看到某基因訊號」，真相通常是「這台機器根本沒去測它」，而不是「它不表現」。把「沒測」講成「陰性」是嚴重的邏輯錯誤。 ③ 忽略影像分割 (segmentation) 誤差——成像資料的細胞邊界是靠演算法切出來的。切錯了，一顆細胞的 transcript 會被歸到隔壁細胞，讓兩顆細胞的表達譜互相汙染（尤其細胞擠得密的地方）。空間結果務必回頭對照原始影像，別盡信數字。 Three common pitfalls (rookies fall for all three — read them, dodge them): ① Treating a Visium spot as one cell — the classic mistake. A spot houses several, even a dozen, cells, so its profile is a mixed signal. Run single-cell analysis on a spot as if it were "one cell" and your conclusions get diluted and contaminated. For cell proportions you need deconvolution — and those are estimates, so don't over-interpret boundaries. ② Mistaking "not on the panel" for "not expressed" — imaging only measures the few hundred genes on the panel. "Not seeing a gene's signal" usually means "the instrument never measured it," not "it isn't expressed." Reporting "not measured" as "negative" is a serious logical error. ③ Ignoring segmentation errors — imaging cell boundaries are drawn by an algorithm. A bad cut assigns one cell's transcripts to its neighbor, so two cells' profiles contaminate each other (worst where cells are densely packed). Always cross-check spatial results against the raw image; don't trust the numbers blindly.
