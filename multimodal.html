多模態與免疫圖譜分析

在同一顆細胞裡同時讀取 RNA、表面蛋白 (ADT) 與免疫受體 (TCR/BCR)，並用 hashtag 把多個樣本合併定序後再拆回。一顆細胞、多層真相。

Read RNA, surface protein (ADT), and immune receptors (TCR/BCR) in the same cell — and pool multiple samples with hashtags, then demultiplex them back. One cell, many layers of truth.

概念

一、什麼是「多模態」單細胞？

傳統 scRNA-seq 只量測一種模態：mRNA 表達——它告訴你「這顆細胞想做什麼」。但細胞不只有基因藍圖，它表面掛著什麼蛋白、它的免疫受體長什麼樣、它到底是從哪個病人來的，這些都是同一顆細胞身上的其他真相。多模態 (multimodal) 指在同一顆細胞裡同時捕捉這些不同層次的生物訊息——例如 RNA 加上表面蛋白，或 RNA 加上免疫受體序列。

關鍵在於「同一顆細胞」四個字。因為所有模態都來自同一顆細胞，你可以把「這顆細胞表現哪些基因」與「它表面有哪些蛋白、它的 T/B 細胞受體是什麼」直接對應起來，而不是把兩批不同細胞硬湊在一起猜。這就像同時拿到一個人的「履歷」與「員工證」——兩份文件講的是同一個人，才能交叉比對。

BBrowserX 的多模態模組涵蓋三大主軸：① CITE-seq / ADT（表面蛋白）；② TCR/BCR（免疫受體 repertoire）；③ demultiplexing（樣本 hashing 拆分）。本章逐一說明它們「量什麼、為何有用、常見誤區、在 BBrowserX 怎麼看」，最後告訴你怎麼把它們疊在同一張圖上一起解讀。

Classic scRNA-seq measures one modality: mRNA expression — it tells you what a cell wants to do. But a cell is more than its gene blueprint: which proteins sit on its surface, what its immune receptor looks like, which patient it came from — these are all other truths about the very same cell. Multimodal single-cell captures those different layers from the same cell at once — e.g. RNA plus surface protein, or RNA plus immune-receptor sequence.

The magic is in those two words: same cell. Because every layer comes from the same cell, you can directly link “which genes this cell expresses” with “which proteins sit on its surface” and “what its T/B-cell receptor is” — instead of stitching together two separate batches of cells and guessing. It's like holding both a person's résumé and their ID badge — cross-checking only works when both documents describe the same individual.

The BBrowserX multimodal module covers three pillars: (1) CITE-seq / ADT (surface protein); (2) TCR/BCR (immune-receptor repertoire); and (3) demultiplexing (sample hashing). This chapter walks through what each measures, why it helps, common pitfalls, and how you view it in BBrowserX — then shows how to layer them all on one plot.

一句話記住三者的分工：RNA 告訴你細胞「是誰、想做什麼」；ADT 告訴你它「表面實際掛了什麼」；TCR/BCR 告訴你它「屬於哪個家族、有沒有被抗原點名擴增」；HTO 告訴你它「從哪個樣本來、是不是兩顆黏在一起」。四張拼圖拼起來，才是完整的細胞故事。 One-line division of labor: RNA tells you who a cell is and what it wants to do; ADT tells you what's actually on its surface; TCR/BCR tells you which family it belongs to and whether an antigen has expanded it; HTO tells you which sample it came from and whether it's two cells stuck together. Snap all four puzzle pieces together for the full story.
表面蛋白

二、CITE-seq / ADT：量測表面蛋白

📇
比喻：履歷 vs 名片。RNA 是細胞的「履歷」——它寫著這顆細胞打算合成哪些蛋白（它想做什麼）。ADT 則是細胞的「名片」——它表面實際掛著哪些蛋白（它現在真正的身分）。有時候履歷寫得天花亂墜，名片卻很樸素；有時候履歷平平，名片上頭銜卻很硬。CITE-seq 讓你同時讀這兩份文件，於是你不必再從履歷「猜」名片上會寫什麼。 Analogy: résumé vs business card. RNA is the cell's résumé — it lists which proteins the cell intends to make (what it wants to do). ADT is the cell's business card — which proteins actually sit on its surface right now (who it really is). Sometimes the résumé is flashy but the card is plain; sometimes the résumé is modest but the card carries a heavy title. CITE-seq lets you read both documents at once, so you never again have to guess the card from the résumé.

CITE-seq（Cellular Indexing of Transcriptomes and Epitopes by sequencing）用帶條碼寡核苷酸的抗體去染細胞表面蛋白。想像每種抗體屁股上綁著一小段獨特的 DNA「條碼」，稱為 ADT（Antibody-Derived Tag，抗體衍生標籤）。抗體黏到細胞表面對應的蛋白（例如 CD4 抗體找 CD4 蛋白），沒黏上的洗掉，剩下的 DNA 條碼就跟 mRNA 一起被定序讀出。定序時，某顆細胞上 CD4 的 ADT 計數越高，代表它表面 CD4 蛋白越多。整個過程與 RNA library 平行讀出，互不干擾。

為什麼重要？因為蛋白量不等於 mRNA 量。mRNA 只是「訂單」，蛋白才是「成品」——中間隔著轉譯效率、蛋白半衰期、翻譯後修飾與蛋白週轉率等好幾道關卡。舉個經典例子：許多免疫細胞的 CD4、CD8A 這類表面標記，在 RNA 層次因為表達低、又容易 dropout（該有訊號卻讀成 0），常糊成一團難以分群；但在蛋白層次卻乾淨俐落、呈現清楚的「陰性 vs 陽性」雙峰，一眼就能把 CD4⁺ T 細胞與 CD8⁺ T 細胞分開。ADT 提供更穩定、更接近流式細胞儀 (flow cytometry) 的表面表型讀數，特別適合免疫細胞分型。

在 BBrowserX 中，ADT 會以獨立的「protein / ADT」特徵集載入，可與基因表達並排視覺化——在同一張 UMAP 上切換顯示 CD4 基因 或 CD4 蛋白，或針對 ADT 做 feature plot、violin plot（實際選單名稱依版本略有不同）。

CITE-seq (Cellular Indexing of Transcriptomes and Epitopes by sequencing) stains surface proteins with oligo-barcoded antibodies. Picture every antibody carrying a tiny unique DNA “barcode” on its tail — an ADT (Antibody-Derived Tag). The antibody binds its target protein on the surface (a CD4 antibody hunts CD4 protein), unbound antibody is washed off, and the remaining DNA tags get sequenced right alongside the mRNA. On sequencing, the higher a cell's CD4 ADT count, the more CD4 protein sits on its surface. The whole thing is read out in parallel with the RNA library, without interfering.

Why does it matter? Because protein level ≠ mRNA level. mRNA is just the “order slip”; protein is the finished product — and in between sit translation efficiency, protein half-life, post-translational modification, and turnover. A classic example: surface markers like CD4 and CD8A are lowly expressed and dropout-prone at the RNA level (real signal reads as 0), smearing into an unresolvable blob; yet at the protein level they're crisp and clearly bimodal (negative vs positive), letting you separate CD4⁺ from CD8⁺ T cells at a glance. ADT gives a steadier, flow-cytometry-like surface phenotype — especially handy for immune-cell typing.

In BBrowserX, ADT loads as a separate “protein / ADT” feature set and can be visualized side-by-side with gene expression — toggle CD4 gene vs CD4 protein on the same UMAP, or make ADT feature/violin plots (exact menu names vary by version).

⚠️
常見誤區：ADT 也有「雜訊底噪」與「溢出 (spillover)」。ADT 訊號並非全然乾淨——沒黏好的抗體會留下環境背景 (ambient/background) 訊號，讓「應該是陰性」的細胞出現一點點計數；不同抗體之間也可能互相干擾造成溢出。所以判讀時看的是「相對雙峰結構」而非絕對數字，通常會做 ADT 正規化（如 DSB、CLR）來壓背景。看到少量非零計數別急著喊陽性——先問「它落在雙峰的哪一峰？」 Pitfall: ADT has background noise and spillover, too. ADT signal isn't perfectly clean — unbound antibody leaves an ambient/background floor, so cells that “should” be negative still show a few counts, and antibodies can bleed into one another (spillover). Read the relative bimodal structure, not absolute numbers, and normalize ADT (e.g. DSB, CLR) to suppress background. Don't call a cell positive just because its count is non-zero — first ask which of the two peaks it falls into.

互動 ①：RNA vs 蛋白 (ADT) 的不一致

選一個表面標記，看它在 T 細胞群中的 RNA 訊號（常稀疏、有 dropout，糊成一坨）與 ADT 蛋白訊號（乾淨、雙峰分明）如何不同。留意藍色 RNA 幾乎都擠在「陰性/低」，而綠色 ADT 卻在「高/很高」冒出第二個高峰——那第二峰就是真正的陽性細胞。這正是為什麼加做 ADT 有價值。（合成示意資料）

Pick a surface marker and compare its RNA signal (often sparse, dropout-prone, smeared into one lump) with its ADT protein signal (clean, clearly bimodal) across a T-cell population. Notice the blue RNA bars pile up in “neg/low,” while the green ADT bars raise a second peak at “high/very high” — that second peak is the truly positive cells. This is exactly why adding ADT pays off. (Synthetic illustrative data.)

🎯
實戰情境：CITE-seq 精準分辨相似亞群。假設你有一群 CD4 T 細胞，光靠 RNA 很難把「幼稚 (naïve)」、「記憶 (memory)」與「調節 (Treg)」細分開，因為它們轉錄體很像。這時加上 CD45RA、CD45RO、CD25、CD127 等表面蛋白的 ADT，就像替每顆細胞多發了幾張名片——CD45RA⁺CD45RO⁻ 偏 naïve、CD25 高 CD127 低偏 Treg——原本糊在一起的亞群立刻各就各位。一句話：RNA 定大群，ADT 分細群。 In practice: CITE-seq resolves look-alike subsets. Say you have a blob of CD4 T cells; RNA alone struggles to split naïve, memory, and Treg because their transcriptomes are so similar. Add ADT for surface proteins like CD45RA, CD45RO, CD25, CD127 — like handing each cell extra business cards. CD45RA⁺CD45RO⁻ leans naïve; CD25-high CD127-low leans Treg — and the tangled subsets snap into place. In short: RNA defines the big clusters, ADT splits the fine ones.
免疫受體

三、TCR / BCR：免疫 repertoire

🔎
比喻：受體序列 = 身分證／指紋。每顆 T 細胞的 TCR、每顆 B 細胞的 BCR 序列，都是這顆細胞獨一無二的「身分證字號」。clonotype（選殖型）就是「同一個字號的家族」——來自同一個祖先細胞、複製出來的所有後代都共用同一組受體序列。當某個家族因為抗原刺激大量繁衍，在人群（細胞群）中壯大到隨處可見，就是 clonal expansion（選殖擴增）：像一個原本默默無聞的姓氏，突然變成滿街都是的大家族。 Analogy: the receptor sequence is an ID card / fingerprint. Each T cell's TCR and each B cell's BCR sequence is that cell's one-of-a-kind “ID number.” A clonotype is “the family sharing one ID” — all the descendants copied from a single ancestor cell carry the same receptor sequence. When one family multiplies explosively after antigen stimulation until it's everywhere in the crowd (the cell population), that's clonal expansion: like an obscure surname suddenly becoming a huge clan you see on every street.

每顆 T 細胞的 TCR（T-cell receptor）與每顆 B 細胞的 BCR（B-cell receptor / 抗體）序列，是在發育時經由 V(D)J 重組把 V、(D)、J 幾段基因片段隨機拼接、再加上接點處的隨機插入／刪除拼出來的。這種「隨機洗牌」讓可能的序列組合高達天文數字，因此幾乎每顆淋巴球都有一個獨特的受體序列。單細胞 V(D)J 定序可讀出每一顆細胞的受體序列（連 α/β 或 heavy/light 鏈配對都能保留），共享同一受體序列的細胞就屬於同一個 clonotype（選殖型）。

當某個 clonotype 因為抗原刺激而大量增殖，就出現 clonal expansion（選殖擴增）：少數 clonotype 佔據大部分細胞，整體多樣性 (diversity) 隨之下降。這是判讀免疫反應的核心指標——如果 repertoire 從「一堆小家族、人人平等」變成「少數大家族說了算」，往往代表有抗原專一性的反應正在發生。例如腫瘤浸潤的 T 細胞若出現大幅擴增，通常暗示某些 clone 認得腫瘤抗原、正在被點名放大。

BBrowserX 的威力，在於把 clonotype 連結到轉錄體表型：你可以問「這些大幅擴增的 clone，是不是正好是表現耗竭標記 (PDCD1、HAVCR2、LAG3、TOX) 的耗竭型 CD8 T 細胞？」——這是免疫學裡最有故事的組合：擴增，代表這些 T 細胞曾被強力活化過；耗竭，代表長期慢性刺激把它們榨乾了。把 repertoire（誰是大家族）與 RNA/ADT（這些大家族處在什麼功能狀態）疊在同一群細胞上一起解讀，遠比任何單一模態更有說服力。

Each T cell's TCR and each B cell's BCR (antibody) sequence is assembled during development by V(D)J recombination — randomly splicing V, (D), and J gene segments and adding random insertions/deletions at the junctions. This “shuffle” makes the number of possible sequences astronomical, so nearly every lymphocyte carries a unique receptor. Single-cell V(D)J sequencing reads out the receptor of each individual cell (even preserving the paired α/β or heavy/light chains); cells sharing the same receptor belong to one clonotype.

When a clonotype proliferates after antigen stimulation you get clonal expansion: a few clonotypes dominate the population and overall diversity drops. This is a core readout of immune response — when a repertoire shifts from “many small, egalitarian families” to “a few big families running the show,” an antigen-specific reaction is usually underway. Heavily expanded tumor-infiltrating T cells, for instance, often hint that certain clones recognize tumor antigen and are being amplified.

The power of BBrowserX is linking clonotype to transcriptomic phenotype: you can ask, “Are these heavily expanded clones exactly the exhausted CD8 T cells expressing PDCD1, HAVCR2, LAG3, TOX?” — the most story-rich combination in immunology: expansion means these T cells were once strongly activated; exhaustion means chronic stimulation has worn them out. Overlaying repertoire (who's a big family) with RNA/ADT (what functional state those families are in) on the same cells is far more convincing than any single modality.

💉
實戰情境：免疫治療前後追蹤 T 細胞 clone。因為每個 clonotype 有獨一無二的序列「身分證」，你可以在治療前與治療後兩個時間點抓同一組序列，看它們的命運：某個原本很小的 clone 在打了 anti-PD-1 之後暴增，代表這個 clone 被「解封」重新活化（clonal replacement / expansion），常是有效反應的指標；反之若原本擴增的耗竭 clone 治療後仍原地踏步，可能提示反應不佳。把 TCR 當「條碼」，你就能像追蹤包裹一樣，一路追蹤每個 T 細胞家族在治療旅程中的動態。 In practice: tracking T-cell clones before and after immunotherapy. Because each clonotype has a unique sequence “ID card,” you can grab the same sequences at a pre-treatment and a post-treatment timepoint and follow their fates: a small clone that surges after anti-PD-1 has been “unleashed” and re-activated (clonal replacement/expansion) — often a marker of effective response; conversely, previously expanded exhausted clones that don't budge may signal poor response. Treat the TCR as a barcode and you can track each T-cell family through the treatment journey like tracing a parcel.
⚠️
常見誤區：TCR/BCR 只適用於淋巴球。V(D)J 重組只發生在 T 與 B 細胞——巨噬細胞、樹突細胞、NK 細胞、腫瘤細胞這些沒有受體序列，所以在它們身上找不到 clonotype 是正常的，別誤以為是資料出錯。另一個易踩的坑：偵測到的 TCR 有時只抓到單一條鏈（只有 α 或只有 β），配對不完整；分析擴增時，通常以「完整成對」的 clonotype 較可靠，並注意過濾掉背景污染的低品質 contig。 Pitfall: TCR/BCR applies only to lymphocytes. V(D)J recombination happens only in T and B cells — macrophages, dendritic cells, NK cells, and tumor cells have no receptor sequence, so finding no clonotype in them is expected, not a data error. Another trap: a detected TCR sometimes has only one chain (α or β alone), an incomplete pairing; for expansion analysis, fully paired clonotypes are more reliable, and low-quality/ambient contigs should be filtered out.
樣本合併

四、Demultiplexing / 細胞 hashing

🎫
比喻：演唱會手環。想像好幾場演唱會共用同一個場地同一批工作人員，為了省錢一次辦完。每場觀眾入場時發不同顏色的手環（HTO），然後所有人混進同一個大廳（一次定序 run）。散場時，你只要看每個人手上的手環顏色，就能把他歸回原本那一場——這就是 demultiplexing。更妙的是：如果有人手上戴了兩條不同顏色的手環，那八成是兩個人擠在一起被當成一個計數，也就是 doublet，當場抓出剔除。 Analogy: concert wristbands. Picture several concerts sharing one venue and crew, held together to save money. Each show's audience gets a different-colored wristband (HTO) at entry, then everyone mingles in one big hall (a single sequencing run). Afterward, just read each person's wristband color to send them back to their own show — that's demultiplexing. Even better: anyone wearing two different colors is probably two people counted as one — a doublet — caught and removed on the spot.

細胞 hashing 讓你把多個樣本混在一次定序 run 裡跑，藉此攤平每個細胞的成本、並大幅減少批次效應（因為所有樣本在同一台機器、同一天、同一批試劑下處理）。做法：先用帶有 HTO（Hashtag Oligo，雜交標籤寡核苷酸）的抗體分別標記每個樣本——每個樣本一種「手環顏色」——混合後一起上機。每個 HTO 對應一個樣本身分。

定序後執行 demultiplexing（去多工／拆樣本）：依每顆細胞的 HTO 計數判斷它來自哪個樣本。這裡有個「買一送一」的好處——如果一顆 barcode（液滴）同時偵測到兩種以上 HTO 都明顯高於背景，代表它可能是把來自不同樣本的兩顆細胞包在同一滴裡的 doublet，可以直接標記剔除；只偵測到極低 HTO 的則為 negative（空液滴 / 環境沾附）。也就是說，hashing 不只幫你省錢分樣本，還順手幫你抓了一部分 doublet。

BBrowserX 支援載入並執行 demultiplexing，把每顆細胞指派回原樣本並標記 doublet／negative，讓你在後續分群、比較不同樣本時，都建立在「乾淨、來源明確」的細胞上。

Cell hashing lets you run several samples in one sequencing run, spreading per-cell cost and sharply cutting batch effects (all samples ride the same machine, same day, same reagent lot). How: label each sample with an antibody carrying a distinct HTO (Hashtag Oligo) — one “wristband color” per sample — pool them, and load together. Each HTO encodes one sample identity.

After sequencing you run demultiplexing: each cell's HTO counts reveal its sample of origin. Here's the buy-one-get-one bonus — if one barcode (droplet) shows two or more HTOs clearly above background, it likely wrapped two cells from different samples into one droplet: a doublet, flagged and removed. Barcodes with almost no HTO are negatives (empty droplets / ambient). So hashing doesn't just save money and split samples — it catches a chunk of doublets for free.

BBrowserX supports loading and running demultiplexing, assigning each cell back to its sample and flagging doublets/negatives, so your downstream clustering and cross-sample comparisons rest on clean, source-known cells.

🔀 一顆 barcode 的 HTO 判讀邏輯

問→這個 barcode 偵測到幾種 HTO 明顯高於背景？（也就是手上戴了幾條手環？）How many HTOs are clearly above background for this barcode? (How many wristbands is it wearing?)
0→Negative：沒戴手環——空液滴或環境訊號，多半剔除。Negative: no wristband — empty droplet / ambient, usually discarded.
1→Singlet：一條手環——指派回該 HTO 對應的樣本 ✅。Singlet: one wristband — assign to that HTO’s sample ✅.
≥2→Doublet：戴了兩條以上不同手環——兩顆（不同樣本）細胞混在一滴，標記剔除。Doublet: two or more different wristbands — two cells (different samples) in one droplet, flag/remove.
⚠️
常見誤區：HTO doublet 偵測是「互補」而非「取代」計算式 doublet 工具。Hashing 只能抓到「來自不同樣本」的異源 (heterotypic) doublet——因為它們戴了兩條不同顏色手環才會露餡。但若兩顆細胞來自同一樣本（同一種 HTO、同一種手環顏色），HTO 看起來就跟一顆單細胞一模一樣，完全無法分辨。這類「同樣本 doublet」仍需仰賴以轉錄體為基礎的計算式方法（如 scDblFinder / DoubletFinder），它們是靠「這顆細胞的表達譜看起來像兩種細胞的混合」來揪出可疑者。兩者角度不同、互相補位——搭配使用最穩。 Pitfall: HTO doublet detection complements — not replaces — computational doublet tools. Hashing only catches heterotypic doublets from different samples — they give themselves away by wearing two colored wristbands. But two cells from the same sample (same HTO, same wristband color) look identical to a single cell; HTO can't tell them apart. Such “same-sample doublets” still need transcriptome-based tools (scDblFinder / DoubletFinder), which flag suspects because “this cell's expression profile looks like a mix of two cell types.” Different angles, mutually covering — use both together for the most robust result.
整合

五、把四種模態疊成一個故事

多模態真正的甜蜜點，不在於「多量了幾種東西」，而在於疊圖之後浮現的完整敘事。把四種模態放到同一張 UMAP 上，每一層各司其職，最後拼出單一模態永遠說不清的故事。以下是典型的整合工作流：

The real sweet spot of multimodal work isn't “we measured a few more things” — it's the complete narrative that emerges once you overlay everything. Put all four modalities on one UMAP, let each layer play its role, and you assemble a story no single modality could ever fully tell. Here's a typical integration workflow:

用 RNA 定義細胞狀態

先用全轉錄體分群、註釋，畫出 UMAP，標出大類（T、B、髓系…）與功能狀態（活化、耗竭、增殖）。RNA 是無偏的地圖底稿。

Cluster and annotate on the whole transcriptome, draw the UMAP, mark broad types (T, B, myeloid…) and functional states (activated, exhausted, cycling). RNA is your unbiased base map.

用 ADT 確認並細分表型

疊上 CD4/CD8 等表面蛋白，把 RNA 分不開的相似亞群（naïve / memory / Treg）用乾淨的蛋白雙峰切開。RNA 定大群，ADT 分細群。

Overlay surface proteins (CD4/CD8…) to split RNA-indistinguishable subsets (naïve / memory / Treg) via clean protein bimodality. RNA for big clusters, ADT for fine ones.

用 TCR/BCR 找出擴增 clone

把 clonotype 大小映射到 UMAP，看哪些細胞屬於大幅擴增的家族。問：這些大 clone 落在哪個 RNA/ADT 定義的群？

Map clonotype size onto the UMAP to see which cells belong to heavily expanded families. Ask: where do the big clones sit relative to RNA/ADT-defined clusters?

用 HTO 確認來源與純度

用樣本身分回答「這個發現在多個病人／時間點都成立嗎？」並確保分析建立在剔除 doublet 後的乾淨細胞上。

Use sample identity to answer “does this hold across patients/timepoints?” and ensure the analysis rests on clean, doublet-removed cells.

🧩
整合的結論長這樣：「這一群細胞，RNA 顯示是耗竭特徵、ADT 確認是 CD8 蛋白陽性、TCR 顯示它們屬於少數幾個大幅擴增的 clone、HTO 確認它們主要來自治療前的腫瘤樣本。」——這樣一句話，四個模態互相佐證，比任何單一資料都硬。這就是多模態存在的理由。 An integrated conclusion reads like this: “This cluster shows an exhaustion signature by RNA, is confirmed CD8-protein-positive by ADT, belongs to a few heavily expanded clones by TCR, and comes mainly from the pre-treatment tumor sample by HTO.” Four modalities corroborating each other in one sentence — far sturdier than any single dataset. That's the whole reason multimodal exists.
