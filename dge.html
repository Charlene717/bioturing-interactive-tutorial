STEP 6 · 差異表達與 Venice

差異表達分析與 Venice 演算法

找出區分細胞群或條件的 marker 基因——並理解為何 BioTuring 用「分佈」而非「平均」來定義差異。

Find the marker genes that distinguish a cluster or condition — and learn why BioTuring compares whole distributions, not just means.

核心直覺

平均值會說謊

先講一個小故事。有兩個班級要考同一份數學考卷，最後全班平均都是 60 分。校長只看成績單上的「平均」，一句話就下結論：「這兩班程度一樣。」——但真的一樣嗎？

走進教室你才發現：A 班每個人都考 58～62 分，整整齊齊擠在 60 分附近；B 班卻是一半的人考 0 分、另一半考 100 分，中間幾乎沒人。兩班的平均一模一樣，實際狀況卻天差地遠：一班是「大家都懂一半」，另一班是「一半人全懂、一半人全不懂」。這時候如果你只拿「平均」去比，會很有自信地說「沒差異」——而這個結論大錯特錯。

這就是本頁的核心：平均值（mean）是一個會說謊的摘要。它把整群的複雜樣貌壓縮成一個數字，遇到「混合」的群體時，這個數字誰都不代表。而單細胞資料，恰恰就是最愛出現這種「B 班」的地方。

Start with a little story. Two classes sit the same math exam, and both end up with a class average of 60. The principal glances at the mean on the report and declares: "These two classes are equally strong." — but are they, really?

Walk into the rooms and you'll see the truth. In Class A everyone scored between 58 and 62, packed tightly around 60. In Class B, half the students scored 0 and the other half scored 100, with almost no one in between. The two means are identical, yet the realities could not be more different: one class is "everyone half-understands," the other is "half know everything, half know nothing." Compare them by the mean alone and you'll confidently announce "no difference" — a conclusion that is flatly wrong.

That is the heart of this page: the mean is a summary that can lie. It squeezes a group's rich shape into one number, and when the group is a mixture, that number represents no one. And single-cell data is exactly where "Class B" shows up all the time.

🧠
把班級換成細胞：一個「class」就是一群細胞（一個 cluster 或一個實驗條件），每個「學生的分數」就是一個細胞裡某基因的表達量。B 班那種「一半 0、一半 100」在單細胞裡超常見——因為一群細胞往往是多種狀態的混合體（有些細胞開了這個基因、有些完全關著）。所以：要看差異，得看整條分佈，不能只看平均。 Now swap classes for cells: a "class" is a group of cells (a cluster or a condition), and each "student's score" is one gene's expression in one cell. That "half-0, half-100" pattern is everywhere in single cell, because a cell group is usually a mixture of states (some cells have the gene switched on, others fully off). So: to see a difference, look at the whole distribution — not just the mean.
目標

一、差異表達分析在單細胞中的目標

分群與註釋之後，一個關鍵問題是：是什麼基因讓這一群細胞與眾不同？差異表達分析（Differential Expression, DE）就是要找出這些 marker 基因——在某個 cluster（或某個實驗條件）中，表達顯著高於（或低於）其他細胞的基因。你可以把它想成幫每一群細胞找出「身分證上的特徵」：CD3 高 → 大概是 T 細胞；MS4A1 高 → 大概是 B 細胞。

在 BBrowserX 裡，DE 有兩個典型用途：① Cluster marker——把一群 vs. 其餘所有細胞比較，得到定義該群的基因（也是自動註釋的依據）；② Condition comparison——把同一種細胞型在「處理 vs. 對照」「腫瘤 vs. 正常」之間比較，找出反應基因。操作上通常是：選取細胞群 → 選擇比較對象 → 選演算法 → 產生排序後的基因清單。

舉個實戰數字：假設你有一個 12,000 顆細胞、18,000 個基因的資料集，你選出第 5 群（約 900 顆）去和其餘 11,100 顆比較，BBrowserX 會回傳一張每個基因一列的表——含效應大小（log2FC）、顯著性（p 值），你再排序、篩選，通常挑前 10～30 個 marker 來認這群到底是誰。

After clustering and annotation, a central question remains: which genes make this group of cells distinct? Differential expression (DE) analysis finds those marker genes — genes expressed significantly higher (or lower) in one cluster (or one experimental condition) than in the rest. Think of it as printing each group's "ID-card features": high CD3 → probably T cells; high MS4A1 → probably B cells.

In BBrowserX, DE serves two typical purposes: (1) Cluster markers — comparing one group vs. all remaining cells to get the genes that define it (this also feeds auto-annotation); (2) Condition comparison — comparing the same cell type between "treated vs. control" or "tumor vs. normal" to find responding genes. The workflow is usually: select a cell group → choose what to compare against → pick a test → get a ranked gene list.

A concrete number: say you have a dataset of 12,000 cells and 18,000 genes, and you pick cluster 5 (~900 cells) to compare against the remaining 11,100. BBrowserX returns a table with one row per gene — effect size (log2FC) and significance (p-value) — which you then sort and filter, usually taking the top 10–30 markers to work out who this cluster really is.

Run "this group vs. the rest," and the top markers come back as NKG7, GNLY, KLRD1 — cytotoxic genes — so you can confidently label it NK cells (natural killers). DE is the step that translates "a blob of dots" into "a cell type with a name."
方法選擇

二、標準統計檢定與 Venice

BBrowserX 同時提供傳統的統計檢定與 BioTuring 自研的 Venice。你可以把它們想成三種「不同視力」的檢查員：

  • t-test——只量兩班的平均分數（mean）差多少，假設分數大致呈鐘形（近似常態 normal）。就像只看兩班平均分，不管分數怎麼分佈。
  • Wilcoxon rank-sum（Mann–Whitney U）——無母數（non-parametric）的秩檢定，比的是「把兩班的人排排站，A 班的人普遍站得比 B 班前面嗎？」也就是分佈的位置（中位數傾向）。它對離群值（outlier）比較不敏感（少數幾個超高分不會把它帶歪），是 scRNA-seq 常見預設。
  • Venice——BioTuring 自研，不看單一數字，而是把整條表達分佈攤開來一起比較——形狀、峰數、比例通通看。就像那個真的走進兩間教室、把每個人的分數都看過的老師。

BBrowserX offers both classical statistical tests and BioTuring's proprietary Venice. Picture them as three inspectors with different "eyesight":

  • t-test — only measures how far apart the two class means are, assuming scores are roughly bell-shaped (approximately normal). Like reading two class averages and ignoring how the scores spread.
  • Wilcoxon rank-sum (Mann–Whitney U) — a non-parametric rank test that asks "if we line everyone up, does Class A generally stand ahead of Class B?" — i.e. the location (median tendency) of the distribution. It's less sensitive to outliers (a few very high scores won't skew it) and is a common scRNA-seq default.
  • Venice — BioTuring's own method. Instead of one number, it lays out the whole expression distribution and compares everything — shape, number of peaks, proportions. Like the teacher who actually walked into both rooms and looked at every single score.
⚠️
為什麼「平均值」不夠？傳統方法（Seurat、CellRanger、EdgeR）以「平均表達」定義差異。但一群細胞往往是多種細胞型／狀態的混合——例如一半細胞完全不表達某基因、另一半高度表達（正是我們的「B 班」）。這種群體的「平均值」誰都不代表。單一參數無法描述整個群體，這正是 Venice 想解決的問題。記住那句話：混合群體 → 平均值失真 → 需要看分佈。 Why isn't the mean enough? Conventional methods (Seurat, CellRanger, EdgeR) define DE by comparing mean expression. But a cell population is often a mixture of cell types/states — say half the cells don't express a gene at all while the other half express it highly (that's our "Class B"). The "mean" of such a population represents no one. A single parameter can't describe the whole population — exactly the problem Venice targets. The mantra: mixed population → mean distorts → look at the distribution.

Venice 能辨識的四種差異型態

因為 Venice 比較的是整條分佈，它能區分出四類差異——其中三類是純平均檢定容易漏掉的。每一類都附一個「一句話定義 + 迷你例子」，幫你一眼記住：

Because Venice compares the whole distribution, it distinguishes four kinds of difference — three of which pure mean-based tests tend to miss. Each comes with a one-line definition and a tiny example to make it stick:

DE

平均差異

兩組的平均表達不同——最經典的差異。

Classic DE: the two groups differ in mean expression.

例：A 平均 3.5、B 平均 6.5。整體都上調了。e.g. A averages 3.5, B averages 6.5 — shifted up overall.
DP

比例差異

「表達 vs. 不表達」的細胞比例改變了，峰的位置沒變。

The fraction of expressing vs. silent cells changes, while the peak positions stay put.

例：A 有 35% 細胞開了某基因，B 變成 60%。e.g. 35% of A's cells switch the gene on, but 60% of B's do.
DM

型態差異

分佈的形狀／峰數不同——單峰 vs. 雙峰，平均卻可能一樣。

The shape / number of peaks differs — unimodal vs. bimodal — even if the means match.

例：A 集中在 5 附近，B 一半在 2、一半在 8（我們的 B 班）。e.g. A clusters near 5, B splits half at 2 and half at 8 (our Class B).
DB

型態＋平均

同時有型態差異又有平均差異（DM + DE），最複雜的一種。

Both differential modality and differential expression at once — the most complex case.

例：A 單峰在 4；B 雙峰且整體也往上偏到平均 7。e.g. A is unimodal at 4; B is bimodal and also shifted up to a mean of 7.
🏆
Venice 的實績：在模擬資料上與 15 種方法比較，Venice 取得最佳 AUC（AUC 越接近 1，代表越能把「真的有差異」和「沒差異」分辨清楚）。它是開源、學術免費的，內建於 BioTuring 的 Signac 套件中。論文：Nguyen et al., Venice: A New Algorithm for Finding Marker Genes in Single-Cell Transcriptomic Data, bioRxiv 2020, DOI 10.1101/2020.11.16.384479。 Venice's track record: benchmarked against 15 methods on simulated data, Venice achieved the best AUC (the closer AUC is to 1, the better it separates "truly different" from "not different"). It is open-source and free for academics, built into BioTuring's Signac package. Paper: Nguyen et al., Venice: A New Algorithm for Finding Marker Genes in Single-Cell Transcriptomic Data, bioRxiv 2020, DOI 10.1101/2020.11.16.384479.
🔬
專家小撇步——為什麼 scRNA-seq 特別需要看分佈？單細胞資料充滿「零」：因為技術限制（沒抓到那個 mRNA），或因為那顆細胞真的沒表達，很多基因在很多細胞裡都是 0。這種「一堆 0 加上一小撮高值」天生就是雙峰、天生就是混合。這正是 DP 與 DM 型態的溫床——也正是只看平均的檢定最容易翻船的地方。 Pro tip — why scRNA-seq especially needs distributions: single-cell data is full of zeros — either technical (the mRNA wasn't captured) or true (the cell really doesn't express it). Many genes read 0 in many cells. This "pile of zeros plus a small cluster of high values" is inherently bimodal, inherently mixed — a breeding ground for DP and DM patterns, and exactly where mean-only tests capsize.

用一句話收束：t-test 看「平均」，Wilcoxon 看「位置」，Venice 看「整條分佈」。視野越寬，能抓到的真實差異就越多——但也別忘了，看得越細，你越需要對結果的生物意義做判斷（見第六節）。

In one line: t-test looks at the "mean," Wilcoxon at the "location," Venice at the "whole distribution." The wider the view, the more real differences you catch — but remember, the finer the lens, the more you must judge the biological meaning of what it finds (see Section VI).

🌳 我該選哪個檢定？

想要→快速、穩健的 cluster marker 預設？用 Wilcoxon（多數 scRNA 流程的預設）。a fast, robust default for cluster markers? Use Wilcoxon (the default in most scRNA pipelines).
擔心→群體是混合細胞型／雙峰表達，怕漏掉分佈型態差異？用 Venice。the group is a mixture / bimodal and you'd miss distributional differences? Use Venice.
需要→與教科書／舊分析對齊、假設近常態？可用 t-test，但留意其侷限。to align with textbook/legacy analyses under near-normal assumptions? t-test works, but mind its limits.
解讀

五、看懂結果：log2FC、adjusted p 與火山圖

跑完 DE，你會拿到一張長長的表。看懂它只要抓兩個數字：「差多少」（效應大小）和「多有把握」（統計顯著性）。這兩件事分別對應 log2FC 和 adjusted p 值——它們回答不同問題，缺一不可。

After DE you get a long table. Reading it comes down to two numbers: "how big is the difference" (effect size) and "how sure are we" (statistical significance). These map to log2FC and the adjusted p-value — they answer different questions, and you need both.

📈

log2 Fold Change

log2FC 是表達倍率變化取以 2 為底的對數：+1 代表 A 組表達為 B 組的 2 倍、+2 為 4 倍、+3 為 8 倍、−1 為一半。它衡量效果大小（方向與幅度），正值＝在該群上調、負值＝下調、0＝沒變。
為什麼取 log？因為 2 倍和一半在生物上「一樣大」但方向相反，取 log2 之後剛好是 +1 與 −1，對稱好讀。

log2FC is the fold change on a base-2 log scale: +1 means A is 2× B, +2 is 4×, +3 is 8×, −1 is half. It measures effect size (direction & magnitude); positive = up-regulated, negative = down, 0 = unchanged.
Why log? Because "2×" and "half" are equally large in opposite directions — on the log2 scale they become a tidy, symmetric +1 and −1.

🎯

校正後 p 值

單細胞一次檢定數千個基因，必須做多重檢定校正（如 Benjamini–Hochberg FDR）。為什麼？假設你測 18,000 個基因、就算全都沒差異，用 p<0.05 也會純粹靠運氣冒出約 900 個「假陽性」。校正就是把這種「檢定太多次」的僥倖扣掉。請用 adjusted p（q 值）而非原始 p 篩選，常見門檻 < 0.05。它衡量的是統計顯著性，不是效果大小。

Single-cell DE tests thousands of genes at once, so you must apply multiple-testing correction (e.g. Benjamini–Hochberg FDR). Why? Test 18,000 genes and, even if none truly differ, a p<0.05 cutoff throws up about 900 "false positives" by luck alone. Correction discounts that "too-many-tests" fluke. Filter on the adjusted p (q-value), not the raw p — a common cutoff is < 0.05. It measures statistical significance, not effect size.

🌋
火山圖 (Volcano plot)：把 log2FC（x 軸）對 −log10(adjusted p)（y 軸）作圖。名字很浪漫——因為圖形常像一座噴發的火山：中間一團「沒差異」的基因構成火山口，兩側往上噴出的點就是有意義的 marker。x 軸越往右＝在該群上調越多、越往左＝下調越多；y 軸越高＝越顯著。所以右上角（大幅上調且高度顯著）與左上角（大幅下調且高度顯著）的點，就是最值得注意的 marker。BBrowserX 產出的 DE 表可直接排序或畫成火山圖。 Volcano plot: plot log2FC (x-axis) against −log10(adjusted p) (y-axis). The name is fitting — the shape often looks like an erupting volcano: a crater of "no-difference" genes in the middle, with meaningful markers spraying up the sides. Right on x = more up-regulated, left = more down; higher on y = more significant. So points in the upper-right (strongly up and highly significant) and upper-left (strongly down and highly significant) are the markers most worth your attention. ⚠️
兩個常見陷阱：
① 統計顯著 ≠ 生物重要。單細胞動輒上萬個細胞，樣本一多，微小到沒意義的差異也能有極小的 p 值。譬如某基因 log2FC 只有 0.05（幾乎沒變）卻因為 12,000 顆細胞而 q 值 < 0.001——顯著，但生物上根本不重要。務必同時看 log2FC 的幅度與生物脈絡，別被超小 p 值沖昏頭。
② Pseudoreplication（偽重複）。當你比較「條件」（如 patient vs. control）時，若把每個細胞當成獨立樣本，等於把「樣本數 n」從「3 個病人」灌水成「幾萬顆細胞」，p 值會嚴重高估、假陽性爆量。真正的重複單位是個體（病人），不是細胞。跨樣本比較時，應以樣本（個體）為統計單位——常見作法是 pseudobulk：先把每位病人同型細胞的表達加總成一個「假 bulk」樣本，再做組間比較。 Two common pitfalls:
(1) Statistical significance ≠ biological importance. With tens of thousands of cells, a big sample size makes even trivial differences yield tiny p-values. A gene with log2FC of just 0.05 (barely moved) can still hit q < 0.001 thanks to 12,000 cells — significant, but biologically meaningless. Always read the magnitude of log2FC alongside biological context; don't be dazzled by a microscopic p.
(2) Pseudoreplication. When comparing conditions (e.g. patient vs. control), treating each cell as an independent sample inflates your "n" from "3 patients" to "tens of thousands of cells," grossly overstating significance and flooding you with false positives. The true replicate unit is the individual (patient), not the cell. For cross-sample comparisons the statistical unit should be the sample (individual) — commonly via pseudobulk: sum each patient's same-type cells into one "pseudo-bulk" sample, then compare across groups.
從清單到故事

六、拿到 marker 清單之後：怎麼變成生物學結論

DE 表本身只是原料，不是結論。一份漂亮的 marker 清單，還要經過幾道「加工」才會變成能寫進論文的生物學故事。把它想成從「一籃食材」到「一道菜」的過程：

A DE table is raw material, not a conclusion. A pretty marker list needs a few more "processing" steps before it becomes a biology story you can write into a paper. Think of it as going from "a basket of ingredients" to "a finished dish":

① 排序與篩選

先用 adjusted p < 0.05 濾掉雜訊，再依 log2FC 排序。通常取正向 top 10～30 當該群的招牌 marker。祕訣：同時要求「表達細胞比例（pct）夠高」——一個只在 3% 細胞出現的基因，就算 log2FC 很大也不太適合當群體 marker。

First filter out noise with adjusted p < 0.05, then sort by log2FC. Usually take the top 10–30 positive genes as the group's signature markers. Tip: also require a high "percent expressing (pct)" — a gene seen in only 3% of cells is a poor group marker even with a big log2FC.

② 用小提琴圖／熱圖驗證

別只信數字。把 top marker 畫成 violin plot（小提琴圖）或 heatmap（熱圖），親眼確認它真的只在目標群「亮」起來。這一步常常能揪出「其實在好幾群都高」的假 marker——正好呼應本頁主題：看形狀，別只看數字。

Don't trust numbers alone. Draw top markers as violin plots or a heatmap and confirm they really "light up" only in the target group. This step often exposes fake markers that are actually high in several groups — echoing this page's theme: look at the shape, not just the number.

③ 認出細胞型或功能

把 top marker 對照已知的細胞型標記（如 CD3D→T、LYZ→單核球）或功能程式（如 interferon 反應基因一起上升）。BBrowserX 的自動註釋與 MetaReference（建於 1 億+ 細胞）可以幫你加速這一步。

Match top markers against known cell-type markers (e.g. CD3D→T cells, LYZ→monocytes) or functional programs (e.g. a coordinated rise in interferon-response genes). BBrowserX's auto-annotation and MetaReference (built on 100M+ cells) can accelerate this step.

④ 富集分析放大格局

單一基因說一個字，一群基因才說一句話。把上調 marker 丟進基因集富集（GSEA / pathway enrichment），看看它們是否集中在某條路徑（如「T 細胞活化」「氧化磷酸化」），這才是能寫進 abstract 的層級結論。

One gene says a word; a set of genes says a sentence. Feed the up-regulated markers into gene-set enrichment (GSEA / pathway enrichment) to see if they concentrate in a pathway (e.g. "T-cell activation," "oxidative phosphorylation") — that's the abstract-worthy, higher-level conclusion.

🧭
一句話帶走：DE 找出「哪些基因不同」，但「不同得有沒有意義」要靠你把效應大小、表達比例、視覺化、與生物脈絡兜在一起判斷。工具給你候選，科學家給結論。 One takeaway: DE tells you "which genes differ," but "whether the difference matters" is your call — made by weaving together effect size, expressing fraction, visualization, and biological context. The tool gives candidates; the scientist gives conclusions.
