STEP 4 · 分群與批次校正

分群與批次校正

用 Louvain 把細胞分成群、用 resolution 控制粒度，並用 Harmony 整合多個批次——理解每個按鈕背後的演算法。

Cluster cells with Louvain, tune granularity with resolution, and integrate multiple batches with Harmony — understanding the algorithm behind each button.

先抓住大方向

這一步在整條流程裡的位置

想像你剛辦完一場幾千人的大型派對——每位賓客就是一顆細胞 (cell)。前面幾步（品管、標準化、降維）已經幫每位賓客量好身高、記下穿著、整理成一張「個人特徵卡」。現在最有趣的問題來了：這一屋子的人，其實可以自動分成哪幾個「聊得來的小圈圈」？這就是分群 (clustering) 要回答的事。

分群不是隨便貼標籤，而是整條單細胞分析的樞紐：分得好，後面的細胞註釋、差異表達、軌跡分析才站得穩；分得糟，等於一開始就把桌次排錯，後面全盤皆錯。而當你把好幾場派對（多個樣本／批次）合在一起辦時，還會遇到「不同攝影棚打光不同」的麻煩——同一種人在不同棚裡看起來竟然不一樣，這就需要批次校正 (batch correction) 出馬。這一頁就把這兩件事一次講透。

Picture a party with a few thousand guests — each guest is a cell. The earlier steps (QC, normalization, dimensionality reduction) already measured everyone's height, noted their outfit, and compiled a personal "trait card." Now comes the fun question: which "cliques that vibe together" does this crowd naturally fall into? That is what clustering answers.

Clustering is not random labeling — it is the hinge of the whole single-cell analysis. Get it right and the downstream annotation, differential expression, and trajectory work stand on solid ground; get it wrong and it is like seating everyone at the wrong tables, so everything after collapses. And when you throw several parties (multiple samples / batches) into one room, you hit the "different studios light the set differently" problem — the same kind of person looks different from studio to studio, which calls for batch correction. This page covers both, end to end.

🎉

派對分圈圈

分群 = 讓一屋子賓客自動聚成「聊得來的小圈圈」，每個圈圈就是一個推定細胞群。

Clustering = letting the crowd self-organize into "cliques that click." Each clique is a putative cell population.

🔍

放大鏡倍率

Resolution = 放大鏡倍率。調高看到更多小圈圈，調低就併成大團體——沒有唯一正確倍率。

Resolution = the magnifier's zoom. Turn it up to see more small cliques, down to merge into big groups — no single correct zoom.

💡

統一打光

Harmony = 把不同攝影棚的燈光統一，讓「真正的差異」才被看見，而不是被打光騙了。

Harmony = unifying the studio lighting so only real differences show through, instead of being fooled by the lighting.

為什麼要分群

一、分群：從降維空間找出細胞群

做完 標準化與降維（Step 3） 之後，每個細胞都被壓縮成 PCA 空間 (主成分空間) 中的一個點——你可以想像成把每位賓客濃縮成一張「幾十個數字」的座標卡。分群 (clustering) 的任務，就是把「座標相近、表現相似」的細胞聚在一起，得到一個個推定的細胞群 (putative cell populations)。「推定」兩字很重要：這些群此刻還沒有名字，只是「第 1 群、第 2 群⋯⋯」，得等到下一步 細胞註釋 才會用 marker gene 認出它們是 T 細胞、B 細胞還是巨噬細胞。

那電腦到底怎麼「看出」哪些人是一圈的？BBrowserX 在背後採用 Louvain 社群偵測演算法 (Louvain community detection)，作用在 PCA 空間上建構的 k-最近鄰圖 (kNN graph)。用派對來比喻，整件事分成三步：

After normalization & dimensionality reduction (Step 3), each cell is squeezed into a single point in PCA space — think of it as boiling each guest down to a coordinate card of a few dozen numbers. Clustering groups cells whose coordinates (and thus expression) are similar, yielding putative cell populations. The word "putative" matters: these groups have no names yet — just "Cluster 1, Cluster 2…" — until the next step, cell annotation, uses marker genes to recognize them as T cells, B cells, or macrophages.

So how does the computer actually "see" who belongs together? Under the hood, BBrowserX uses the Louvain community-detection algorithm on a k-nearest-neighbor (kNN) graph built in PCA space. In party terms, it happens in three steps:

建立 kNN 圖：每個人牽起最聊得來的鄰居

在 PCA 空間中，為每個細胞找出離它最近的 k 個鄰居（例如 k=15、20），彼此牽一條線，就織成一張圖 (graph)——節點是細胞，邊代表「你們很像」。這一步只看「誰跟誰最像」，還沒開始分群。

In PCA space, find each cell's k nearest neighbors (say k=15 or 20) and draw an edge between them, weaving a graph — nodes are cells, edges mean "you two are alike." This step only records who resembles whom; no grouping yet.

社群偵測 (Louvain)：自動找出哪些人其實屬於同一群

Louvain 在這張人際網上，找出「圈內牽線密、圈外牽線疏」的社群 (community)——也就是內部彼此都認識、對外卻很少往來的小團體。每一個這樣的社群，就是一個群。它靠最佳化一個叫 modularity（模組度） 的分數來衡量「分得夠不夠乾淨」。

On this social web, Louvain finds communities that are densely wired inside and sparsely wired outside — tight-knit groups that all know each other but barely mingle across. Each such community is a cluster. It does this by maximizing a score called modularity, a measure of how cleanly the split separates dense groups from sparse links.

群 = 推定細胞群：等著被命名

得到的每個群，都是一個「還沒命名」的推定細胞群，等著在下一步用 marker gene 註釋成 T 細胞、B 細胞、NK 細胞⋯⋯。此刻的顏色標籤只是編號，別急著賦予生物意義。

Each resulting cluster is an unnamed putative population, waiting to be annotated (T cell, B cell, NK cell…) via marker genes in the next step. The color labels right now are just IDs — don't rush to read biology into them.

💡
重要觀念：分群發生在 PCA 空間，不是在 UMAP 上。UMAP 只是把高維結果攤平畫出來看的 2D 視覺化，像是把立體地球硬壓成平面地圖——會失真。即使兩個群在 UMAP 上看起來擠在一起，它們仍是 Louvain 在高維空間中判定的獨立社群；反過來，UMAP 上兩坨看起來分很開的細胞也未必真的差很多。永遠以 marker gene，而不是 UMAP 上的距離，來下最終判斷。 Key point: clustering happens in PCA space, not on the UMAP. UMAP is only a 2D flattened rendering of a high-dimensional result — like squashing a globe onto a flat map, distortion is inevitable. Two clusters that look squeezed together on the UMAP are still separate communities Louvain identified in high-dimensional space; conversely, two blobs that look far apart on the UMAP may not differ much. Always make the final call from marker genes, not from distances on the UMAP.
🎯
為什麼重要：分群是整條流程的「分桌」動作。這一步的每一個群，都會被當成一個獨立的細胞型送進註釋、差異表達與軌跡分析。分錯了，後面所有結論都建立在錯誤的桌次上——所以值得多花點時間把它調好、驗好。 Why it matters: clustering is the "seating the tables" moment of the pipeline. Every cluster here gets treated as a distinct cell type by annotation, differential expression, and trajectory analysis downstream. Seat people wrong and every later conclusion rests on the wrong tables — so it is worth the extra time to tune and validate.
互動

二、Resolution 參數：控制分群粒度

Louvain 有一個最關鍵的旋鈕：resolution（解析度）。它就像放大鏡的倍率——調高倍率，原本一大團的圈圈會被看穿成好幾個小圈圈（群越多、每群越小）；調低倍率，這些小圈圈又會併回一個大團體（群越少、每群越大）。技術上，resolution 調整的是 Louvain 最佳化 modularity 時「多在意小社群」的權重。

拖動下方滑桿，看看同一批細胞在不同 resolution 下被切成幾群。注意：細胞的位置完全沒變，變的只是「你用多細的顏色去切它」——這正是重點：群數是你的選擇，不是資料的鐵律。

Louvain has one crucial knob: resolution. Think of it as a magnifier's zoom — zoom in and one big clique gets seen through as several small ones (more, smaller clusters); zoom out and those small ones merge back into a big group (fewer, larger clusters). Technically, resolution tunes how much Louvain "cares about" small communities when maximizing modularity.

Drag the slider below to see how the same cells split into different numbers of clusters. Notice the cell positions never move — only how finely you color-cut them. That is exactly the point: the number of clusters is your choice, not a hard fact of the data.

⚠️
沒有唯一正確的 resolution。不要盲目相信預設值（BBrowserX 常見預設約 0.8）。若群被切得太細，可能把一個真實細胞型硬拆成好幾群（例如把一種 T 細胞誤分成三群）；太粗則可能把兩種不同細胞混成一群。正確做法是用已知 marker gene 驗證：一個「乾淨」的群，內部應該有一致的 marker 表現；如果一群裡有些細胞高表現 CD4、有些高表現 CD8，那它八成該再切開。 There is no single correct resolution. Don't blindly trust the default (BBrowserX often defaults around 0.8). Too high and one real cell type may be forced apart into several clusters (e.g., one T-cell type split into three); too low and two distinct types may merge. The fix is to validate with known marker genes: a clean cluster should show consistent marker expression inside. If some cells in a cluster are high for CD4 and others high for CD8, it probably needs splitting.
🧭

專家小撇步：掃描而非猜測

別執著於「找那一個神奇數值」。實務上會掃描一段範圍（例如 0.4、0.8、1.2）各跑一次，再挑「marker 最說得通、群數最穩定」的那一檔。穩定 = 稍微改 resolution，主要的群結構不會大變。

Don't chase one magic value. In practice you sweep a range (say 0.4, 0.8, 1.2), run each, and pick the one where markers make the most sense and the structure is most stable — stable meaning a small resolution change doesn't scramble the main clusters.

🩺

實戰情境：稀有細胞被吞掉

你在找一群只佔 1% 的稀有細胞（例如漿細胞樣樹突細胞）。在低 resolution 下它被併進大群、消失無蹤；把 resolution 調高後，它才「浮」出來成為獨立一群。稀有族群往往需要較高 resolution 或針對性再分群才看得見。

You're hunting a rare population that's only 1% of cells (say plasmacytoid dendritic cells). At low resolution it gets absorbed into a big cluster and vanishes; raise the resolution and it finally "surfaces" as its own cluster. Rare populations often need higher resolution or targeted subclustering to appear.

操作與再分群

三、在 BBrowserX 中怎麼做 + 再分群

好消息是：以上這些高深的圖論與最佳化，在 BBrowserX 裡都被收進幾個按鈕。在分析選單中，分群通常是「一鍵」執行 Louvain 並自動幫細胞上色標群；你可以在介面上調整 resolution 後重新分群，即時看 UMAP 上的顏色重新洗牌。這正是無程式碼 (no-code) 平台的價值——把「建 kNN 圖 → Louvain → 調 resolution」這串演算法，變成拖一下、按一下就完成。

但有時你會遇到一種尷尬狀況：某一群明明用了合理 resolution，marker 卻不一致——它其實是個混合群（例如一群「T 細胞」裡藏著 CD4 與 CD8 兩種）。這時暴力地把全域 resolution 一路調高並不划算，因為那會順便把其他本來就分好的群也切碎。更精準的做法是再分群 (subclustering / reclustering)：把那一群單獨拿出來，只在這群內部重新建 kNN 圖、重跑一次 Louvain，把它切得更細——像是把某一桌客人單獨拉到一間小房間再重新分組，完全不打擾其他桌。

The good news: all that graph theory and optimization is folded into a few buttons in BBrowserX. In the analysis menu, clustering is typically a one-click Louvain run that automatically colors and labels the cells; you can adjust resolution and re-cluster from the interface and watch the UMAP colors reshuffle live. That is exactly the value of a no-code platform — the whole "build kNN graph → Louvain → tune resolution" chain becomes a drag and a click.

But sometimes you hit an awkward case: a cluster uses a reasonable resolution yet its markers are inconsistent — it's really a mixture (e.g., a "T-cell" cluster hiding both CD4 and CD8 cells). Brute-forcing the global resolution higher is a bad deal here, because it also shatters the other clusters that were already fine. The precise move is subclustering / reclustering: take that cluster on its own, rebuild the kNN graph within just those cells, and rerun Louvain to split it finer — like pulling one table of guests into a side room to regroup, without disturbing anyone else.

🌳 我的群需要再分群嗎？

Q1→這一群的 marker gene 是否不一致（不同細胞表現不同 marker）？是 → 可能是混合群，考慮再分群。否 → 這群大概已經乾淨，收工。Are the cluster's markers inconsistent (different cells express different markers)? Yes → likely a mixture, consider subclustering. No → it's probably clean, done.
Q2→先試著提高 resolution 全域重跑，這一群會自然分開、又不把別的群切爛嗎？會 → 不必單獨再分群。Try raising resolution globally first — does the cluster split on its own without shredding the others? Yes → no separate subclustering needed.
Q3→若只有這一群要更細、其他群不動 → 用 subclustering 只針對它重新分群，再回頭用 marker 確認切出來的子群各自乾淨。If only this cluster needs finer resolution while others stay put → use subclustering on that cluster alone, then re-check with markers that each subcluster is clean.

怎麼判斷分得好不好

四、怎麼判斷分群分得好不好？

初學者最常問：「那我怎麼知道我分對了？」殘酷的事實是——沒有「正確答案」可以對照，這是非監督式 (unsupervised) 分析的本質。但這不代表全憑感覺；有幾個實用的健檢清單，能讓你對「分得好不好」很有底氣：

The question every beginner asks: "How do I know I got it right?" The hard truth — there is no answer key to check against, because this is unsupervised analysis. But that doesn't mean flying blind; a few practical health checks give you real confidence in whether the clustering is good:

🧬

① 每群有一致、專屬的 marker

最重要的一條。跑差異表達，看每群是否有幾個明確的 top marker，而且群內細胞大多都表現它。若一群找不出任何專屬 marker，它可能只是雜訊或該被併掉。

The single most important check. Run DE and see whether each cluster has a few clear top markers that most of its cells express. A cluster with no exclusive markers may be noise or should be merged.

🎨

② 生物邊界對得上

把已知的關鍵基因（如 CD3、MS4A1、LYZ）畫在 UMAP 上，群的邊界應該大致對得上這些基因的高／低表現分界。對得越齊，越可信。

Overlay known key genes (CD3, MS4A1, LYZ) on the UMAP; cluster boundaries should roughly align with the high/low expression borders of those genes. The tighter the alignment, the more trustworthy.

⚖️

③ 對 resolution 穩定

稍微上下調 resolution，主要的群應該還在、只有邊緣細節微變。若一動就天翻地覆，代表這個切法很脆弱、不可靠。

Nudge resolution up and down; the major clusters should persist with only minor edge changes. If everything reshuffles at the slightest tweak, that split is fragile and unreliable.

🚫

④ 排除技術假象

確認某群不是被 QC 沒清乾淨的東西撐起來的——例如高粒線體比例（將死細胞）、doublet，或（多樣本時）純粹的批次。這些會偽裝成「一群」，讓你誤以為找到新細胞型。

Make sure a cluster isn't propped up by leftover QC issues — high mitochondrial fraction (dying cells), doublets, or (with multiple samples) pure batch. These masquerade as "a cluster" and can fool you into thinking you found a new cell type.

🧠
心法一句話：好的分群不是「群數最多」或「UMAP 最漂亮」，而是每一群都能用生物學講出一個合理的故事。當你能對每個群說出「這是⋯⋯細胞，因為它高表現⋯⋯」，才算真的分好了。 The one-line rule: good clustering isn't "the most clusters" or "the prettiest UMAP" — it's when every cluster tells a sensible biological story. Once you can say "this is a ___ cell, because it's high in ___" for each cluster, you've truly clustered well.
批次校正

五、批次校正 / 整合：Harmony

現在把場景放大：你不只辦一場派對，而是把好幾場派對（多個樣本 / 批次）——不同病人、不同定序日、甚至不同實驗室的資料——合在同一個房間分析。麻煩來了：這些派對是在不同攝影棚拍的，每個棚打光不同。結果同一種細胞（比如同一種 T 細胞），只因為「來自哪個棚」，在資料上看起來竟然不一樣。這種純粹由技術造成、與生物無關的系統性差異，就叫 批次效應 (batch effect)。

批次效應從哪來？常見來源包括：不同定序批 (run)、不同批號的試劑 (reagent lot)、不同平台 / 機台、不同操作日期或人員、樣本處理與保存差異等。若不處理，細胞很可能依「來自哪個批次」而非「是哪種細胞」分群——UMAP 上你會看到每個批次各自縮成一座孤島，而不是同型細胞不分批次地聚在一起。這時做出來的「群」，其實是批次，不是生物。

BBrowserX 支援 Harmony 做批次校正 / 整合。它的角色，就是那位把不同攝影棚燈光統一的燈光師：目標很明確——移除技術性的批次差異，同時保留真正的生物差異，讓「真正的不同」才被看見。切換下方按鈕，親眼比較 Harmony 校正前後的差別。

Now zoom out: you're not running one party but merging several parties (multiple samples / batches) — different patients, different sequencing days, even different labs — into one room to analyze together. Here's the snag: those parties were shot in different studios, each with different lighting. So the same cell type (say, one kind of T cell) ends up looking different in the data purely because of which studio it came from. That systematic, technology-driven, biology-irrelevant difference is the batch effect.

Where do batch effects come from? Common sources: different sequencing runs, different reagent lots, different platforms / machines, different dates or operators, and differences in sample handling and storage. Left untreated, cells tend to cluster by which batch they came from rather than which cell type they are — on the UMAP you'll see each batch shrink into its own island instead of same-type cells grouping across batches. The "clusters" you'd get are batches, not biology.

BBrowserX supports Harmony for batch correction / integration. Its role is the lighting director who unifies every studio's lighting: the goal is explicit — remove the technical batch difference while preserving the true biological difference, so only what's really different shows. ⚠️
過度校正 (over-correction) 的危險。燈光師若「調過頭」，把光打得太平，會連被拍的人本身真實的差異也一起抹掉。Harmony 校正過頭時同理——可能把本該分開的細胞硬拉在一起。具體情境：你比較腫瘤與正常組織，兩者剛好各是一個批次。若 Harmony 校正過度，它會把「腫瘤 vs 正常」這個真實的生物差異誤當成批次效應一起消掉——結果腫瘤細胞和正常細胞被硬混成同一群，你研究的核心差異就這樣不見了。整合後務必檢查兩件事：① 不同批次是否混合良好；② 已知該分開的細胞型是否仍然分得開。批次效應是否需要校正、要校正到什麼程度，是一個判斷題，不是永遠都要開的開關。 The danger of over-correction. A lighting director who "overdoes it" and flattens the light will also wipe out the real differences among the people. Same with an over-aggressive Harmony — it can force cells that should stay apart to merge. Concrete scenario: you compare tumor vs normal tissue, and each happens to be its own batch. Over-correct and Harmony mistakes the real biological "tumor vs normal" difference for a batch effect and removes it too — tumor and normal cells get forced into the same cluster, and the very difference you're studying disappears. After integration, always check two things: ① do the batches mix well; ② do cell types that should stay separate still stay separate? Whether — and how strongly — to correct is a judgment call, not an always-on switch.
🩺
實戰情境：先問「值不值得校」。如果你所有樣本都是同一天、同一批試劑、同一台機器跑的，批次效應可能很小，貿然開 Harmony 反而是「治療沒生的病」。反之，跨病人、跨定序日的大型整合資料，批次效應通常明顯，該校。判斷起點：先不校正跑一次分群、依批次上色看 UMAP——如果各批次已經混得很好，就不必動手；如果各成孤島，才輪到 Harmony。 In the field: first ask "is correction even worth it?" If all your samples ran on the same day, same reagent lot, same machine, the batch effect may be tiny, and firing up Harmony "treats a disease you don't have." Conversely, large integrations across patients and sequencing days usually have obvious batch effects worth correcting. Starting point: cluster once without correction, color the UMAP by batch — if batches already mix well, leave it alone; if they form islands, then Harmony's turn has come.
常見誤區

六、常見誤區與破解

分群與批次校正這一步，最容易踩的坑往往不是技術操作，而是心態。把這幾個常見誤區記在心裡，能省下大量事後重做的時間：

The biggest traps in clustering and batch correction usually aren't about buttons — they're about mindset. Keeping these common pitfalls in mind will save you a lot of rework later:

🔬

誤區①：盲目追求最多群

「群越多是不是越厲害？」——不是。把 resolution 一路拉滿，會得到一堆沒有專屬 marker、彼此難以區分的碎群，反而讓下游註釋變成惡夢。破解：以「每群都講得出生物故事」為上限，而非以群數多寡自豪。

"More clusters = more impressive?" No. Cranking resolution to the max yields a pile of tiny clusters with no exclusive markers, indistinguishable from each other, turning downstream annotation into a nightmare. Fix: cap it at "every cluster tells a biological story," not at a high cluster count.

🧯

誤區②：過度整合

「反正 Harmony 開了穩妥。」——不。過度整合會抹平真實差異（見上一節的腫瘤 vs 正常）。破解：先看批次是否真的分離再決定校不校，整合後一定要驗「該分開的還分得開嗎」。

"Just turn Harmony on to be safe." No. Over-integration erases real biology (see the tumor-vs-normal case above). Fix: check whether batches actually separate before correcting, and after integrating always verify "do the things that should stay apart still stay apart?"

🗺️

誤區③：拿 UMAP 距離下結論

「這兩群在 UMAP 上很近，應該很像吧？」——不一定。UMAP 是壓扁後的視覺化，群間距離不能當量尺。破解：相似度用 marker 與差異表達判斷，UMAP 只當導覽圖。

"These two clusters are close on the UMAP, so they must be alike?" Not necessarily. UMAP is a flattened view; inter-cluster distance isn't a ruler. Fix: judge similarity by markers and DE; treat the UMAP as a map, not a measuring tape.

🎯

誤區④：一次分群定終身

「跑一次就完事。」——分群其實是迭代的：分群→看 marker→發現混合群→再分群或調 resolution→再驗證。第一次的結果只是草稿。破解：把它當一個來回打磨的循環，而非一次性動作。

"Run it once and move on." Clustering is actually iterative: cluster → inspect markers → spot a mixture → subcluster or retune → re-validate. The first pass is just a draft. Fix: treat it as a refine-and-repeat loop, not a single action.
