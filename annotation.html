STEP 5 · 細胞註釋

細胞類型註釋

用 BBrowserX 的 MetaReference 自動預測細胞類型，再以 marker 基因驗證——這是 BioTuring 的招牌強項。

Auto-predict cell types with BBrowserX's MetaReference, then validate with marker genes — a signature BioTuring strength.

為什麼重要

分群之後，該幫每群戴上「職業名牌」

想像你走進一場擠滿上千人的大型研討會，主辦單位很偷懶——每個人胸前的名牌只印了 0 號、1 號、2 號…。你知道他們自然地分成了幾群人聚在一起聊天，但你完全不知道誰是誰、誰做什麼工作。這正是分群 (clustering) 做完之後的狀態：細胞被切成一團團，卻沒有任何生物學意義。

細胞註釋 (cell annotation) 就是那個「幫每一群沒有名字的細胞戴上職業名牌」的步驟——把 cluster 0 認出來是「T 細胞」、cluster 3 是「單核球」、cluster 7 是「內皮細胞」。這一步聽起來很像貼標籤的瑣事，實際上卻是整個分析的分水嶺：從這裡開始，你的數字終於變成了會說故事的生物學。

BBrowserX 提供兩條互補的路徑幫你貼名牌：①自動預測 (MetaReference)——一鍵把你的細胞丟去比對 BioTuring 的巨型參考資料庫，像「刷臉」一樣秒認身分；②marker 基因手動註釋——親自檢查每一群的標記基因（marker gene）表達，對照經典 marker 後手動命名。最佳實務是兩條路一起走：自動預測給你一份「草稿名單」節省九成時間，marker 驗證則確保這份草稿沒有認錯人。

Picture yourself walking into a conference hall packed with a thousand people, and the organisers were lazy — everyone's badge just reads #0, #1, #2…. You can see people have naturally clumped into a few chatting groups, but you have no idea who anyone is or what they do. That is exactly where you stand right after clustering: cells are grouped into blobs, yet those blobs carry no biological meaning.

Cell annotation is the step where you "pin a job-title badge on every nameless cluster of cells" — recognising cluster 0 as "T cells," cluster 3 as "monocytes," cluster 7 as "endothelial cells." It sounds like tedious labelling, but it is actually the watershed of the whole analysis: from here on, your numbers finally turn into biology that tells a story.

BBrowserX offers two complementary paths for pinning those badges: (1) automatic prediction (MetaReference) — one click sends your cells off to match BioTuring's giant reference database, recognising identities in a flash like "face-scanning"; and (2) marker-based manual annotation — you personally inspect each cluster's marker gene expression and name it against canonical markers. Best practice is to walk both paths together: auto-prediction hands you a "draft list" that saves 90% of the time, while marker validation makes sure that draft did not mistake anyone's identity.

💡
心法：把自動預測當作「一位很快但偶爾出錯的實習助理」——它幫你省下 90% 的時間，但最終掛上名牌、簽下名字的責任仍在你身上。永遠用 marker 基因與生物學脈絡再確認一次，這才是專業與外行的分野。 Mindset: treat auto-prediction as "a fast but occasionally-wrong intern" — it saves you 90% of the time, but the final call, the badge with your signature on it, is yours. Always re-confirm with marker genes and biological context; that habit is what separates a pro from an amateur.
🏷️

沒名牌 = 沒故事

「cluster 4 在疾病組變多」毫無意義；「調節性 T 細胞在疾病組變多」才是能寫進論文的發現。

"Cluster 4 increases in disease" means nothing; "regulatory T cells increase in disease" is a publishable finding.

🧭

下游全靠它

差異表達、細胞比例、細胞通訊……每一項分析都以「這群是什麼細胞」為前提。

DE, cell proportions, cell–cell communication — every downstream analysis is premised on "what cell type is this."

⚖️

錯一個，全盤錯

貼錯一張名牌，後面所有結論都繼承這個錯誤，而且很難事後察覺。

A single mislabel is inherited by every later conclusion — and it's hard to catch after the fact.

路徑一：自動

一、MetaReference 自動細胞類型預測

MetaReference 是 BBrowserX 最閃亮的招牌功能。用一個生活化的比喻：它就像機場那台「刷臉比對一個上億人資料庫、秒認出你是誰」的閘門。它把你的每一顆細胞，拿去和 BioTuring 精心整理的超過 1 億 (100M+) 顆單細胞參考資料庫比對，直接告訴你這顆細胞的身分——目前可分類 54 種細胞類型與 183 種亞型 (subtype)，而且這個名單還在持續擴增。BBrowserX Pro 版更進一步提供癌細胞預測 (cancer cell prediction)，這在腫瘤研究中格外實用。

背後的核心概念叫做參考式標籤轉移 (reference-based label transfer)。拆開來講很直覺：參考庫裡的每顆細胞都已經被專家「戴好名牌」了；演算法把你的細胞投影到同一個參考空間，找出「表達模式長得最像」的參考鄰居，然後把鄰居的名牌轉移 (transfer) 到你的細胞上。這其實就是一種 最近參考匹配 (nearest-reference matching)——你的細胞借用了最相似參考細胞的身分。

這和路徑二的手動註釋有一個根本差異：手動註釋是「由下而上」——你親眼看 marker 表達、憑生物學知識做判斷；自動預測則是「由上而下」——直接借用一個龐大既有圖譜的集體智慧。兩者的優劣，我們在第三節會攤開來比。

MetaReference is BBrowserX's most eye-catching feature. Here's a down-to-earth analogy: it's like the airport gate that "face-scans against a database of a billion people and recognises you in a second." It takes each of your cells and matches it against BioTuring's carefully curated reference of over 100 million (100M+) single cells, telling you the identity directly — currently classifying 54 cell types and 183 subtypes, and that roster keeps expanding. BBrowserX Pro goes a step further with cancer cell prediction, especially handy in tumour research.

The core concept behind it is reference-based label transfer. Unpacked, it's intuitive: every cell in the reference has already been given a name badge by experts; the algorithm projects your cells into that same reference space, finds the reference neighbours whose "expression pattern looks most alike," and transfers the neighbour's badge onto your cell. This is essentially nearest-reference matching — your cell borrows the identity of the most similar reference cell.

This differs fundamentally from the manual annotation of Path 2: manual annotation is "bottom-up" — you look at marker expression with your own eyes and judge from biological knowledge; auto-prediction is "top-down" — it borrows the collective wisdom of a vast existing atlas. We lay out the trade-offs in Section III.

開啟預測功能

在分群完成的專案中，找到細胞類型預測 / MetaReference 功能（實際選單名稱會因版本而異）。

In a clustered project, open the cell type prediction / MetaReference tool (exact menu name varies by version).

執行預測（刷臉）

軟體把你的資料比對參考庫，替每個細胞或每群產生預測標籤與信心分數 (confidence score)——分數就是「這張臉有多確定認對了」。

The software matches your data to the reference and produces a predicted label plus a confidence score per cell or cluster — the score says "how sure it is the face matched."

覆蓋到 UMAP

將預測標籤上色到 UMAP，直覺檢視哪些群落被指派為哪種細胞類型。好的預測應該「一群一色、界線乾淨」；若一群裡混了三四種顏色，那就是警訊。

Colour the predicted labels onto the UMAP to visually inspect which clusters got which cell type. A good prediction should be "one cluster, one colour, clean borders"; a cluster splattered with three or four colours is a red flag.

驗證再定案

務必用 marker 基因（路徑二）與生物學脈絡確認後，再把預測結果採納為正式標籤。這一步不能跳過——理由見第三節那顆紅色警示。

Always confirm with marker genes (Path 2) and biological context before adopting predictions as final labels. This step is non-negotiable — see the red warning in Section III.

⚠️
參考偏誤 (reference bias)：自動預測的品質受限於參考庫涵蓋的細胞類型——刷臉比對只能認出「資料庫裡登記過的人」。罕見、新穎或組織特異的細胞，很可能被硬塞成「長得最像的既有類型」。若你的樣本含參考庫少見的族群，請把低信心分數的預測當作提示 (lead) 而非結論 (conclusion)。 Reference bias: auto-prediction quality is bounded by what the reference covers — face-scanning can only recognise "people already registered in the database." Rare, novel, or tissue-specific cells are easily forced into "the nearest existing type." If your sample contains populations underrepresented in the reference, treat low-confidence predictions as leads, not conclusions.
路徑二：手動

二、Marker 基因手動註釋

如果自動預測是「刷臉」，那 marker 基因手動註釋就是「看制服和識別證認人」。Marker 基因是某種細胞類型高度、又專一表達的基因——就像消防員的紅色制服、醫師的白袍、警察的臂章。只要你看到某群細胞集體「穿著」T 細胞的制服，你就能自信地把它命名為 T 細胞。

實際做法：對每個 cluster 檢視經典 marker 的表達——用 violin plot（小提琴圖）、dot plot（點圖）、feature plot（在 UMAP 上把某基因的表達上色） 觀察——若某群強烈表達某細胞類型的招牌 marker，就把它命名為該類型。BBrowserX 的 Cell Search 還能反過來操作：依表達模式搜尋細胞（例如「找出 CD3D 高、CD79A 低的細胞」），直接把符合某套表達邏輯的細胞挑出來。

下面這張表是每位單細胞分析者都該背在腦中的「制服對照表」。注意 PTPRC(CD45) 和 EPCAM 這種「大類分界」marker：CD45 是所有免疫細胞的總稱識別證，EPCAM 則標記上皮——用它們可以先把細胞粗分成免疫 vs. 非免疫兩大陣營，再往下細分。

If auto-prediction is "face-scanning," then marker-based manual annotation is "recognising people by their uniform and ID card." Marker genes are genes a cell type expresses highly and specifically — like a firefighter's red uniform, a doctor's white coat, a police officer's armband. Once you see a cluster collectively "wearing" the T-cell uniform, you can confidently name it a T cell.

In practice: for each cluster, inspect canonical marker expression — via violin plots, dot plots, and feature plots (colouring a gene's expression on the UMAP) — and if a cluster strongly expresses a cell type's hallmark markers, name it that type. BBrowserX's Cell Search also works the other way: find cells by expression pattern (e.g. "cells high in CD3D, low in CD79A"), pulling out cells that match an expression logic.

Note the "big-branch divider" markers like PTPRC (CD45) and EPCAM: CD45 is the umbrella ID card for all immune cells, while EPCAM marks epithelium — use them to first split cells into immune vs. non-immune camps, then subdivide.

怎麼挑一個「好」marker？

不是每個「在某細胞高表達」的基因都能當 marker。一個真正好用的識別證，要同時滿足兩個條件：

Not every gene that "runs high in some cell type" makes a good marker. A truly useful ID card must meet two conditions at once:

🎯

特異性（只有它有）

這個基因應該幾乎只在目標細胞高表達，別的細胞都很低。CD3D 只在 T 細胞亮、其他群幾乎是黑的——這才是理想識別證。反例：管家基因 ACTB、GAPDH 到處都高，等於「人人都穿的白 T 恤」，完全認不出人。

The gene should run high almost only in the target cell, staying low elsewhere. CD3D lights up in T cells and is nearly black in others — that's an ideal ID. Counter-example: housekeeping genes like ACTB, GAPDH are high everywhere, a "plain white T-shirt everyone wears" — useless for telling people apart.

📶

穩定表現（別忽亮忽暗）

好 marker 應在該細胞大多數個體都穩定表現，而不是只有零星幾顆亮。表現太稀疏、或在不同樣本間忽有忽無的基因，容易讓你的判斷隨機漂移。優先選在該群「覆蓋率高、又夠亮」的基因。

A good marker should be stably present in most cells of that type, not just a scattered few. Genes that are too sparse, or that flicker on and off across samples, make your calls drift randomly. Prefer genes that are both "widely covered and bright enough" within the cluster.

🔬
專家小撇步：永遠用一組 (a panel) marker、而非單一基因下判斷。T 細胞就同時看 CD3D + CD3E；B 細胞就 MS4A1 + CD79A。多個識別證一致指向同一身分，你的信心才站得住腳。單靠一張識別證，遇到造假（技術噪音、doublet）就翻車。 Pro tip: always decide with a panel of markers, never a single gene. For T cells look at CD3D + CD3E together; for B cells, MS4A1 + CD79A. When several ID cards point to the same identity, your confidence stands on solid ground. Bet on one card alone and you'll crash the moment a forgery (technical noise, a doublet) shows up.
工作流程

三、招牌流程：自動先給草稿 → marker 驗證 → 命名

把兩條路徑串成一條聰明的產線，就是 BioTuring 使用者最推薦的工作流。核心口訣是：「自動先給草稿，marker 負責驗證，最後你來命名。」

Chaining the two paths into one smart pipeline is the workflow BioTuring users recommend most. The mantra: "auto drafts, markers validate, and you name."

自動先給草稿

先跑 MetaReference，一鍵得到全資料集的預測標籤與信心分數。這份「草稿名單」讓你不必從零開始猜，把注意力集中在真正可疑的地方。

Run MetaReference first to get predicted labels and confidence scores across the whole dataset in one click. This "draft roster" means you don't guess from scratch — you focus attention where it's genuinely uncertain.

marker 逐群驗證

對每群拉出 2–3 個經典 marker（violin/dot/feature plot）核對：草稿說是 T 細胞，那 CD3D/CD3E 有沒有亮？識別證與制服對得上，才算通過驗證。

For each cluster, pull 2–3 canonical markers (violin/dot/feature plots) to check: the draft says T cells — do CD3D/CD3E light up? Only when the ID card matches the uniform does it pass validation.

處理不合格的群

草稿與 marker 不一致、或群內混雜？把它挑出來 subcluster（再分群） 重看，或暫標為 "unknown"。這些通常正是最有故事的族群。

Draft disagrees with markers, or the cluster is mixed? Pull it out to subcluster and re-inspect, or mark it "unknown" for now. These are often exactly the most interesting populations.

最後你來命名

驗證通過後，親手把最終標籤寫上去。命名時盡量用一致的粒度（都用大類、或都細到亞型），別一群叫「T 細胞」、隔壁卻叫「CD8+ 效應記憶 T 細胞」，後續統計會亂。

Once validated, write the final label yourself. Name at a consistent granularity (all broad, or all down to subtype) — don't call one cluster "T cells" and its neighbour "CD8+ effector-memory T cells," or downstream statistics get messy.

🧩
實戰情境：你有一組 PBMC（外周血單核細胞）資料，MetaReference 一鍵貼好 8 個群的名牌，其中 7 群的 marker 一驗就過（CD3D、MS4A1、LYZ… 全都對）。只有第 8 群被標成「T 細胞」卻信心只有 41%。你拉 marker 一看——CD3D 低、但 PPBP 爆高！原來這是一小群血小板 (platelets)，參考庫裡樣本太少才被硬塞成最像的 T 細胞。你把它改名、記錄下來，五分鐘搞定。這就是「自動給草稿、marker 抓漏、你來拍板」的威力。 In the field: you have a PBMC dataset; MetaReference badges 8 clusters in one click, and 7 of them pass marker validation instantly (CD3D, MS4A1, LYZ… all check out). Only cluster 8 is labelled "T cells" at just 41% confidence. You pull markers — CD3D is low, but PPBP is sky-high! It's a small cluster of platelets, forced into the nearest "T cell" because the reference had too few of them. You rename it, document it, done in five minutes. That's the power of "auto drafts, markers catch leaks, you make the call."

🌳 我該怎麼註釋這個資料集？

Q1→先跑 MetaReference 自動預測，取得快速起點與信心分數。Run MetaReference auto-prediction first for a fast starting point plus confidence scores.
Q2→預測信心高？→ 用 2–3 個經典 marker（violin/dot/feature plot）快速驗證即可定案。Prediction high-confidence? → Validate with 2–3 canonical markers (violin/dot/feature plots) and finalize.
Q3→預測信心低或群內混雜？→ 切回 marker 手動註釋；必要時 subcluster 再重新檢視。Prediction low-confidence or mixed? → Fall back to manual markers; subcluster and re-inspect if needed.
Q4→怎麼都對不上任何已知 marker？→ 可能是新族群或雙細胞 (doublet)，標為 "unknown" 並記錄。Matches no known markers at all? → Possibly a novel population or doublet; label "unknown" and document it.
🛑
絕不要跳過驗證——這是最貴的一堂課。只採納自動預測、卻不看 marker，是單細胞分析最常見的錯誤來源之一。設想一個慘痛情境：某罕見的漿細胞樣 (plasmacytoid) 群，因為參考庫代表性不足，被自動預測「刷臉刷錯」硬塞成 T 細胞；你沒驗證就採納，結果整篇論文的「T 細胞在疾病組增加」其實是假象，審稿人一驗 marker 就抓包退稿。錯誤的細胞標籤會污染下游每一項結論——差異表達、細胞比例、細胞通訊全跟著錯。註釋是整個分析可信度的地基。 Never skip validation — it's the most expensive lesson. Adopting auto-predictions without checking markers is among the most common error sources in single-cell analysis. Imagine a painful scenario: a rare plasmacytoid population, underrepresented in the reference, gets "face-scanned wrong" and forced into T cells; you accept it without validating, and now your paper's "T cells increase in disease" is an artefact — a reviewer checks markers, catches it, and rejects the manuscript. A wrong cell label contaminates every downstream conclusion — DE, cell proportions, cell–cell communication all inherit the error. Annotation is the foundation of the whole analysis's credibility.

常見誤區

五、三個最常見的翻車陷阱

就算你懂原理，實作時還是很容易踩進這幾個坑。先認識它們，遇到時才不會慌。

Even when you understand the theory, these pits are easy to fall into during hands-on work. Learn them first so you don't panic when they appear.

1️⃣

一個 marker 就下結論

看到 CD8A 亮就喊「這是 CD8 T 細胞」——但 CD8A 在部分 NK、樹突細胞也會出現。永遠用一組 marker 交叉確認，別讓單一識別證騙了你。

Seeing CD8A light up and shouting "CD8 T cell" — but CD8A also appears in some NK and dendritic cells. Always cross-check with a panel; don't let one ID card fool you.

2️⃣

忽略 doublet 的假中間態

兩顆細胞被誤黏成一顆雙細胞 (doublet)，會同時「穿兩套制服」（如 CD3D + MS4A1 都亮），偽裝成一個看似新奇的「T-B 中間態」。看到不合生物學的混合表達，先懷疑 doublet，別急著發現新細胞。

Two cells stuck together as a doublet "wear two uniforms at once" (e.g. both CD3D + MS4A1 up), masquerading as a novel "T-B intermediate." When you see biologically implausible mixed expression, suspect a doublet before claiming a new cell type.

3️⃣

粒度不一致

一群命名到大類「髓系細胞」，隔壁卻細到「CD16+ 非經典單核球」。混用粒度會讓細胞比例統計失真，命名前先決定好整體要粗還是細。

Naming one cluster broadly as "myeloid cells" while its neighbour goes fine-grained to "CD16+ non-classical monocytes." Mixed granularity distorts proportion statistics — decide broad vs. fine for the whole set before naming.

定案前的自我檢查清單

  • 每一群都有至少 2 個一致的經典 marker 支持了嗎？Does every cluster have at least 2 consistent canonical markers backing it?
  • 自動預測的信心分數都看過了嗎？低分的群額外驗證了嗎？Have you reviewed every cluster's confidence score, and extra-validated the low ones?
  • 有沒有「同時穿兩套制服」的群？排除 doublet 了嗎？Any cluster "wearing two uniforms"? Have you ruled out doublets?
  • 命名粒度一致嗎（全大類 or 全細到亞型）？Is your naming granularity consistent (all broad or all subtype-level)?
  • 對不上任何 marker 的群，有標 unknown 並記錄，而非硬塞嗎？Are unmatchable clusters labelled unknown and documented, rather than forced?
